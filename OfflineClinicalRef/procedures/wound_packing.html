Circulation / Hemorrhage Procedure

Hemostatic dressing + wound packing

Indications

  • Life-threatening bleeding from:
    • Junctional areas (groin, axilla, neck)
    • Deep wounds where direct pressure alone is insufficient
    • Extremity wounds not controlled by pressure and where tourniquet is not possible/appropriate
  • Temporizing control until definitive care

Contraindications / cautions

  • Do not pack:
    • Superficial/minor wounds controllable with standard dressing
    • Open chest wounds intended for chest seals (manage as open pneumothorax)
    • Abdominal evisceration (cover with moist dressing; do not pack organs)
  • Neck wounds: pack with caution to avoid airway compromise; maintain airway vigilance
  • Use appropriate PPE (blood exposure)

Equipment

  • Hemostatic gauze (preferred) OR plain gauze if unavailable
  • Gloves/eye protection
  • Trauma shears
  • Pressure dressing / elastic wrap
  • Suction if significant blood/secretions

Steps (checklist)

1) Expose and identify bleeding source

  • Remove clothing; wipe/clear pooled blood
  • Locate the bleeding point if possible (visualize/palpate)

2) Pack the wound (firm, deep, continuous)

  • Place gauze directly onto the bleeding source
  • Pack firmly into the wound cavity, layer-by-layer, filling all dead space
  • Maintain constant pressure and keep packing until the cavity is tightly filled

3) Hold direct pressure

  • Apply strong, continuous manual pressure over packed wound
  • Hold for the minimum time recommended for your hemostatic product (VERIFY; common products recommend ~3 minutes)
  • If only plain gauze is used, longer pressure may be required

4) Secure with pressure dressing

  • Apply a pressure dressing/elastic wrap to maintain compression
  • Ensure dressing stays tight during movement/transport

5) Reassess and repeat if needed

  • If bleeding continues:
    • Re-pack (remove only if necessary to reach the source; otherwise add more gauze)
    • Reapply pressure
    • Escalate (tourniquet for extremity if applicable; urgent transfer)

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Bleeding controlled (no ongoing soak-through)
  • Dressing remains secure with maintained pressure
  • Patient perfusion stabilizes (as resuscitation continues)

Complications + immediate management

  • Persistent bleeding
    • Ensure packing reaches source; pack tighter/deeper; reapply pressure; escalate
  • Dressing loosens
    • Reinforce wrap; maintain pressure; re-secure for transport
  • Airway compromise (neck wounds)
    • Monitor continuously; be prepared to manage airway; avoid overly bulky anterior neck compression

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Treat shock: keep warm, monitor vitals, consider TXA per protocol if major hemorrhage
  • Do not repeatedly disturb packing once hemostasis achieved
  • Document: site, product used, time applied, response, ongoing bleeding status
