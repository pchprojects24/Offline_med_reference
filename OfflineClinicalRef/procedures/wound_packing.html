Circulation / Hemorrhage Procedure

Hemostatic dressing + wound packing

Indications

  • [Placeholder: Severe hemorrhage from compressible wounds]
  • [Placeholder: Junctional hemorrhage (groin, axilla, neck) where TQ not feasible]
  • [Placeholder: Deep wounds not controlled by direct pressure alone]

Contraindications / cautions

  • [Placeholder: Body cavity wounds (thorax, abdomen) - do not pack blindly]
  • [Placeholder: Eye injuries - do not use hemostatic agents]
  • [Placeholder: Some hemostatic agents are exothermic - know your product]

Equipment

  • [Placeholder: Hemostatic gauze (Combat Gauze, Celox, etc.)]
  • [Placeholder: Plain gauze as alternative/supplement]
  • [Placeholder: Pressure dressing/bandage]
  • [Placeholder: Gloves]

Steps (checklist)

  1. [Placeholder: Expose wound completely, identify bleeding source]
  2. [Placeholder: Remove pooled blood if possible to visualize source]
  3. [Placeholder: Pack gauze directly into wound, starting at bleeding source]
  4. [Placeholder: Pack tightly - fill entire wound cavity]
  5. [Placeholder: Apply direct pressure for minimum 3 minutes (per product)]
  6. [Placeholder: Apply pressure dressing to maintain compression]

Confirmation / success criteria

  • [Placeholder: Hemorrhage controlled]
  • [Placeholder: Dressing remains in place]
  • [Placeholder: No continued bleeding through dressing]

Complications + immediate management

  • [Placeholder: Continued bleeding - repack more aggressively, longer pressure]
  • [Placeholder: Thermal injury (some agents) - be aware of product properties]
  • [Placeholder: Infection risk - antibiotics, surgical debridement later]

Aftercare / monitoring

  • [Placeholder: Do not remove packing in field]
  • [Placeholder: Monitor for bleeding through dressing]
  • [Placeholder: Surgical consultation for wound exploration/closure]
  • [Placeholder: Document type of hemostatic agent used]
