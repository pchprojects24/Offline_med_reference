Airway Procedure

Cricothyrotomy (CICO / last resort)

Indications

  • Cannot oxygenate / cannot ventilate (CICO) after failed less-invasive airway management, e.g.:
    • BVM (two-person + adjuncts + suction) ineffective
    • Supraglottic airway unsuccessful or not possible
    • Intubation unsuccessful and further attempts are unsafe due to oxygenation failure
  • Severe upper airway obstruction where ventilation cannot be achieved by other means

Contraindications / cautions

  • This is a LAST RESORT procedure; perform only if trained/authorized and CICO exists
  • Pediatric considerations are protocol-dependent; many systems use different approaches in younger children (VERIFY local age cutoffs and method)
  • Distorted neck anatomy, massive neck trauma/hematoma: higher risk and difficulty (may still be required if CICO)

Equipment

  • PPE (face shield strongly recommended); suction ready
  • Scalpel (e.g., #10/#11) per kit
  • Bougie (if using bougie-assisted technique) OR tracheal hook/dilator per your kit
  • Cuffed tube appropriate size for cric (kit-dependent) or a small ETT/trach tube per protocol (VERIFY)
  • Syringe for cuff inflation
  • BVM + oxygen
  • Waveform capnography (ETCO2) if available
  • Securing device/ties; dressing

Steps (checklist) — bougie-assisted approach (adult) (VERIFY local protocol)

  1. Declare CICO and commit
    • Call for help; continue best-possible oxygenation attempt while preparing
    • Identify landmarks (laryngeal handshake):
      • Thyroid cartilage → cricothyroid membrane → cricoid cartilage
    • Position neck as tolerated (neutral if trauma)
  2. Incision and membrane entry
    • Make a vertical skin incision over the membrane area if landmarks difficult
    • Make a horizontal stab/incision through the cricothyroid membrane
    • Maintain control of the opening (rotate scalpel or use finger/bougie per technique)
  3. Bougie and tube placement
    • Insert bougie through the membrane into trachea (confirm tracheal feel as trained)
    • Railroad tube over bougie into trachea to appropriate depth
    • Remove bougie; inflate cuff
  4. Ventilate and confirm
    • Ventilate with BVM
    • Confirm placement:
      • Persistent waveform ETCO2 (preferred)
      • Chest rise, bilateral breath sounds (as feasible), improving SpO2/compliance
    • Secure tube firmly
  5. Ongoing management
    • Continue ventilation at appropriate rate; avoid hyperventilation
    • Prepare for urgent evacuation/definitive airway management and surgical care

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Waveform ETCO2 present and sustained
  • Improving oxygenation/ventilation and clinical status
  • Tube secured; minimal leak

Complications + immediate management

  • Misplacement / false passage
    • If no ETCO2 or no chest rise: remove and reattempt only if still CICO; otherwise revert to best oxygenation method available
  • Bleeding
    • Direct pressure where possible; suction; prioritize oxygenation; expedite transfer
  • Subcutaneous emphysema / tube dislodgement
    • Reconfirm frequently; secure well; manage ventilation pressures
  • Esophageal placement (rare with cric but possible)
    • Recognize early (no ETCO2); remove; re-establish airway

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Continuous monitoring: SpO2, ETCO2 (if available), BP, ECG
  • Document:
    • CICO declaration, time of procedure, method, tube type/size, confirmation method, complications
  • Handover:
    • "Surgical airway in place" + confirmation method + ventilation settings/needs
