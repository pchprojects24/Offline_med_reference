Cricothyrotomy (CICO / last resort)

Indications

  • [Placeholder: Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate (CICO) situation]
  • [Placeholder: Complete upper airway obstruction]
  • [Placeholder: Severe facial/oropharyngeal trauma precluding other airways]

Contraindications / cautions

  • [Placeholder: Age <8-10 years - needle cric preferred]
  • [Placeholder: Laryngeal fracture/trauma - relative]
  • [Placeholder: Tracheal transection - if possible, may need lower approach]

Equipment

  • [Placeholder: Scalpel (size 10 or 20 blade)]
  • [Placeholder: Bougie or tracheal hook]
  • [Placeholder: Cuffed 6.0 ETT or dedicated cric tube]
  • [Placeholder: 10mL syringe]
  • [Placeholder: BVM]
  • [Placeholder: Suction]

Steps (checklist)

  1. [Placeholder: Identify cricothyroid membrane (below thyroid cartilage, above cricoid)]
  2. [Placeholder: Stabilize larynx with non-dominant hand]
  3. [Placeholder: Make vertical skin incision]
  4. [Placeholder: Make horizontal stab through membrane]
  5. [Placeholder: Rotate scalpel or use hook to maintain opening]
  6. [Placeholder: Insert bougie or tube]
  7. [Placeholder: Inflate cuff, ventilate]
  8. [Placeholder: Confirm placement]

Confirmation / success criteria

  • [Placeholder: ETCO2 waveform]
  • [Placeholder: Chest rise with ventilation]
  • [Placeholder: SpO2 improvement]
  • [Placeholder: Bilateral breath sounds]

Complications + immediate management

  • [Placeholder: Hemorrhage - apply pressure, continue procedure]
  • [Placeholder: False passage - redirect, confirm with ETCO2]
  • [Placeholder: Posterior tracheal wall injury - recognize subcutaneous emphysema]
  • [Placeholder: Subglottic stenosis (late) - surgical follow-up]

Aftercare / monitoring

  • [Placeholder: Secure tube]
  • [Placeholder: Continuous ETCO2 and SpO2 monitoring]
  • [Placeholder: Surgical consultation for definitive airway management]
