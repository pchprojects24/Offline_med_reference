Breathing Procedure

Chest tube insertion (tube thoracostomy)

Indications

  • Pneumothorax with significant symptoms, large pneumothorax, or pneumothorax in a patient requiring positive-pressure ventilation
  • Hemothorax (especially traumatic) requiring drainage
  • Tension pneumothorax refractory to temporizing measures or recurring after needle decompression
  • Ongoing air leak/respiratory compromise where definitive pleural drainage is required (per scope/capability)

Contraindications / cautions

  • No absolute contraindication in life-threatening tension physiology when chest tube is required
  • Relative cautions (do not delay lifesaving intervention):
    • Coagulopathy/anticoagulation (bleeding risk)
    • Prior pleural surgery/adhesions (higher complication risk)
    • Unclear anatomy/landmarks (optimize positioning and consider alternate approach/evac if feasible)
  • Do not attempt beyond training/scope; if not within capability, prioritize temporizing measures and urgent transfer

Equipment

  • Sterile chest tube kit/tray:
    • Scalpel, curved clamp/hemostats, forceps, scissors
    • Chest tube(s) appropriate size (VERIFY local sizing)
    • Suture + needle driver; securing device/tape
    • Sterile gloves, gown, drape, antiseptic prep
    • Local anesthetic and syringe/needle if used (VERIFY local meds/dose)
  • Underwater seal drainage system (or local equivalent) + tubing/connectors
  • Suction set-up if used locally (VERIFY settings/use)
  • Occlusive dressing
  • Suction and BVM/oxygen ready
  • Monitoring: ECG, SpO2, BP; ETCO2 if ventilated

Steps (checklist)

1) Prep and position

  • Explain if possible; assign roles; pre-oxygenate and optimize hemodynamics as able
  • Position: supine with ipsilateral arm abducted/overhead if tolerated
  • Identify insertion site:
    • Typically 4th or 5th intercostal space, anterior to mid-axillary line in the "safe triangle" (VERIFY local landmark teaching)
  • Prep skin widely; sterile field and drape
  • Local anesthetic if used and time permits (VERIFY)

2) Incision and blunt dissection

  • Make a horizontal incision over the rib below the intended interspace (common approach)
  • Bluntly dissect with clamp through subcutaneous tissue to the rib
  • Enter pleural space just over the superior aspect of the rib to avoid neurovascular bundle
  • Use finger to confirm pleural entry and sweep for adhesions; maintain tract

3) Insert tube and connect

  • Advance tube into pleural space:
    • Pneumothorax: direct anterior/superior as trained (VERIFY local approach)
    • Hemothorax: direct posterior/inferior as trained (VERIFY local approach)
  • Connect immediately to underwater seal drainage system
  • Confirm function:
    • Clinical improvement, air/blood drainage, appropriate tubing movement ("swinging") or bubbling pattern depending on situation
  • Secure tube with sutures and apply occlusive dressing

4) Confirm placement and reassess

  • Confirm clinically:
    • Breath sounds, chest rise, SpO2, ventilatory compliance, hemodynamics
    • ETCO2 trends if ventilated
  • Obtain imaging confirmation when available (e.g., CXR) without delaying urgent stabilization (VERIFY local expectations)
  • Reassess frequently, especially after movement

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Improved respiratory status and/or hemodynamics
  • Ongoing drainage appropriate to pathology (air/blood) and functional system
  • No escalating subcutaneous emphysema or loss of function
  • Imaging confirmation when available

Complications + immediate management

  • Malposition (subcutaneous, fissure, too shallow/deep)
    • Reassess depth and function; reposition if safe/within scope; consider replacement
  • Bleeding (intercostal vessel, soft tissue)
    • Direct pressure, correct technique over rib, escalate; treat shock
  • Organ injury (lung laceration, diaphragm/abdominal injury)
    • Stop, support ABCs, urgent escalation/transfer
  • Persistent air leak / failure to re-expand
    • Check connections/kinks, dressing seal, tube position; consider additional interventions per protocol
  • Infection
    • Sterile technique; ongoing care and documentation
  • Re-expansion pulmonary edema (rare but serious)
    • Avoid overly rapid drainage when trained to control; treat supportively and escalate (VERIFY local guidance)

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Secure tube well; protect during transfer; keep system below chest level
  • Monitor vitals, SpO2, pain, drainage volume/character, air leak
  • Document:
    • Indication, side, site, tube size, confirmation method, initial output/air release, complications
  • Coordinate definitive care/evac; removal criteria are protocol-dependent (VERIFY)
