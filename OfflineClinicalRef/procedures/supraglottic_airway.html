Airway Procedure

Supraglottic airway (i-gel/LMA)

Indications

  • Need for ventilatory support when BVM is ineffective or difficult
  • Cardiac arrest airway management where SGA is within scope and available
  • Obtunded patient requiring airway support (absent/insufficient protective reflexes), when appropriate per local protocol

Contraindications / cautions

  • Intact gag reflex / awake patient (tolerance/aspiration risk)
  • Severe upper airway obstruction (may not ventilate)
  • Major facial trauma preventing seal/placement (device-dependent)
  • High aspiration risk: treat as higher-risk airway; suction and positioning as able

Equipment

  • Supraglottic airway device (i-gel or LMA) + size options
  • Water-based lubricant
  • BVM + O2 + (optional) PEEP if used locally
  • Suction ready
  • ETCO2 (preferred) + SpO2 monitoring
  • Securing device (tape/holder)
  • If supported/appropriate: gastric tube for devices with gastric channel (per local protocol)

Steps (checklist)

1) Prepare

  • Pre-oxygenate/ventilate with BVM as needed
  • Choose size per manufacturer weight/size guide (use the chart for your device)
  • Inspect device; lubricate posterior surface (avoid excess lube in lumen)

2) Position

  • Neutral/sniffing as tolerated; jaw thrust if needed
  • Open mouth; suction secretions

3) Insert (generic i-gel/LMA principles)

  • Advance along the hard palate with gentle, continuous pressure
  • Do not force; if resistance, withdraw slightly and re-advance with better positioning
  • Insert until definite resistance/"seated" feel (device-dependent)

4) Ventilate and confirm

  • Attach BVM; ventilate with visible chest rise
  • Confirm placement:
    • ETCO2 waveform (preferred)
    • Bilateral breath sounds when feasible
    • Improving SpO2 and compliance
    • Minimal leak at reasonable pressures
  • Secure device

5) Optional gastric channel actions (device-dependent)

  • If device supports and local protocol allows: consider gastric tube placement/decompression
  • Ongoing suction as required

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Effective ventilation with chest rise + stable/improving oxygenation
  • ETCO2 waveform present and consistent (if available)
  • Leak acceptable; ventilation achievable without excessive pressure

Complications + immediate management

  • Leak/poor ventilation
    • Reposition head/jaw; adjust depth; try different size; return to BVM if needed
  • Regurgitation/aspiration
    • Suction; lateral positioning if feasible; reassess airway strategy
  • Airway trauma/bleeding
    • Gentle technique; stop if significant resistance; suction and reassess
  • Dislodgement
    • Reconfirm frequently during movement; secure well

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Continuous SpO2 and ECG; ETCO2 if available
  • Recheck placement after moves, CPR cycles, or changes in compliance
  • Consider escalation to ETT when clinically appropriate and within capability/protocol
  • Document size, confirmation method, and patient response
