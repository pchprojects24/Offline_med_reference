Circulation / Hemorrhage Procedure

Tourniquet application

Indications

  • Life-threatening extremity hemorrhage (arterial or severe venous) that is:
    • Uncontrolled with direct pressure, OR
    • Not amenable to direct pressure (multiple casualties, ongoing threat, limited time), OR
    • Associated with traumatic amputation or near-amputation
  • Use early for massive extremity bleeding when rapid control is required

Contraindications / cautions

  • Not for isolated minor bleeding controllable with direct pressure
  • Not for junctional bleeding (groin/axilla/neck) — use wound packing/hemostatic measures instead
  • Do not place over a joint (ineffective); move proximal
  • Pain is expected; do not loosen for pain alone if bleeding control is required

Equipment

  • Commercial tourniquet (preferred; e.g., windlass type)
  • Marker for time notation
  • Gloves/eye protection
  • Additional dressing for wound as needed

Steps (checklist)

1) Identify life-threatening extremity bleed

  • Expose limb; locate source if feasible without delaying control

2) Apply tourniquet high and tight when time-critical

  • Place tourniquet 5–7 cm (2–3 inches) above bleeding site, between wound and torso
  • If exact wound not visible or multiple injuries: apply "high and tight" on the limb (proximal)
  • Avoid joints; if near a joint, move proximal above the joint

3) Tighten until bleeding stops

  • Tighten strap and windlass until:
    • Bleeding stops AND
    • Distal pulse is absent (if able to check)
  • Secure windlass; do not cover it with bulky dressings that prevent reassessment

4) Document and reassess

  • Write application time visibly (tourniquet label or patient)
  • Reassess regularly:
    • Bleeding control, distal perfusion, device security
  • If bleeding continues:
    • Tighten further OR
    • Apply a second tourniquet proximal to the first

5) Do NOT routinely loosen in the field

  • Leave in place until definitive care unless directed by protocol and the patient is stable with controlled bleeding (protocol-dependent)

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Bleeding controlled
  • Distal pulse absent (when assessable)
  • Tourniquet secured and not slipping

Complications + immediate management

  • Continued bleeding
    • Tighten further; add second tourniquet proximal
  • Slippage/ineffective placement
    • Reposition higher/proximal and retighten
  • Nerve/skin injury (risk increases with time)
    • Prioritize hemorrhage control; document time; expedite definitive care
  • Reperfusion issues if removed improperly
    • Follow local protocol; avoid field removal unless specifically indicated

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Treat shock: keep warm, reassess vitals, rapid evacuation planning
  • Continue to monitor bleeding and tourniquet integrity during movement
  • Document: limb, location, time applied, number of tourniquets, response
