Tourniquet application

Indications

  • [Placeholder: Life-threatening extremity hemorrhage]
  • [Placeholder: Hemorrhage not controlled by direct pressure]
  • [Placeholder: Multiple casualties - rapid hemorrhage control needed]
  • [Placeholder: Traumatic amputation]

Contraindications / cautions

  • [Placeholder: Hemorrhage controllable by direct pressure alone]
  • [Placeholder: Junctional hemorrhage (groin, axilla, neck) - TQ not effective]
  • [Placeholder: Avoid over tourniqueted limbs (burns, frostbite)]

Equipment

  • [Placeholder: Commercial tourniquet (CAT, SOFT-T, etc.)]
  • [Placeholder: Marker to document application time]
  • [Placeholder: Improvised TQ only if commercial not available]

Steps (checklist)

  1. [Placeholder: Apply tourniquet HIGH and TIGHT - proximal to wound]
  2. [Placeholder: Position 2-3 inches above wound, not over joint]
  3. [Placeholder: Pull strap tight, secure through buckle]
  4. [Placeholder: Twist windlass until hemorrhage stops]
  5. [Placeholder: Lock windlass in place]
  6. [Placeholder: Note and document time of application]
  7. [Placeholder: If bleeding continues - apply second TQ above first]

Confirmation / success criteria

  • [Placeholder: Hemorrhage stopped]
  • [Placeholder: No distal pulse (in extremity setting)]
  • [Placeholder: Tourniquet is visible and time documented]

Complications + immediate management

  • [Placeholder: Continued bleeding - apply second TQ above first, tighten]
  • [Placeholder: Ischemic injury (prolonged application) - document time, surgical priority]
  • [Placeholder: Nerve injury - unavoidable, acceptable given life threat]
  • [Placeholder: Reperfusion injury on removal - surgical management]

Aftercare / monitoring

  • [Placeholder: Do NOT remove in field unless directed by medical control]
  • [Placeholder: Document application time prominently]
  • [Placeholder: Keep tourniquet visible - do not cover with blankets]
  • [Placeholder: Surgical consultation for definitive hemorrhage control]
