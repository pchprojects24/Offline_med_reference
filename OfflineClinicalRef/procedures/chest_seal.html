Chest seal / open pneumothorax

Indications

  • [Placeholder: Open (sucking) chest wound]
  • [Placeholder: Penetrating thoracic trauma with visible wound]

Contraindications / cautions

  • [Placeholder: Monitor for tension pneumothorax development]
  • [Placeholder: Be prepared to burp or remove seal if tension develops]

Equipment

  • [Placeholder: Commercial vented chest seal preferred]
  • [Placeholder: Alternative: occlusive dressing (petroleum gauze, plastic wrap)]
  • [Placeholder: Tape]
  • [Placeholder: Gauze for wound cleaning]

Steps (checklist)

  1. [Placeholder: Expose the wound completely]
  2. [Placeholder: Clear debris if able, do not probe]
  3. [Placeholder: Dry surrounding skin if possible]
  4. [Placeholder: Apply vented chest seal over wound during exhalation]
  5. [Placeholder: Ensure seal adheres to all wound margins]
  6. [Placeholder: If using improvised seal - tape 3 sides only to allow venting]
  7. [Placeholder: Check for exit wound - treat same way]

Confirmation / success criteria

  • [Placeholder: No audible air movement through wound]
  • [Placeholder: Improved respiratory status]
  • [Placeholder: Seal remains adherent]

Complications + immediate management

  • [Placeholder: Development of tension pneumothorax - burp seal or needle decompression]
  • [Placeholder: Seal failure - reapply or improvise]
  • [Placeholder: Continued hemorrhage - direct pressure around (not on) seal]

Aftercare / monitoring

  • [Placeholder: Continuous respiratory monitoring]
  • [Placeholder: Chest tube will likely be required]
  • [Placeholder: Monitor for tension physiology]
  • [Placeholder: Surgical consultation for definitive repair]
