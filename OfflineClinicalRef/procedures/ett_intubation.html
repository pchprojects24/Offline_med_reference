Airway Procedure

Endotracheal intubation (ETT)

Indications

  • Failure to oxygenate or ventilate despite basic measures (BVM/adjuncts/SGA)
  • Inability to protect airway (reduced LOC, ongoing vomiting/aspiration risk)
  • Anticipated clinical course requiring a definitive airway (shock, severe head injury, status epilepticus, severe respiratory failure)
  • Cardiac arrest (as part of airway strategy per local protocol)

Contraindications / cautions

  • "Can't intubate / can't oxygenate" (CICO) risk: plan for rescue and surgical airway backup
  • Significant facial/airway trauma, blood/vomit/secretions obscuring view (optimize suction; consider alternate strategy)
  • Hemodynamic instability: induction and positive-pressure ventilation can worsen hypotension (optimize first)
  • Do not persist with repeated attempts if oxygenation is failing — re-oxygenate and change approach

Equipment

  • Airway cart/kit; suction set up and tested (Yankauer + catheter)
  • Oxygen + BVM with reservoir; PEEP if used locally
  • Laryngoscope (video or direct) + blades; spare batteries/light check
  • ETTs (2 sizes) + stylet/bougie; syringe for cuff
  • ETCO2 (waveform preferred), SpO2, monitor/defib
  • OPA/NPA, SGA as backup
  • Securing device/tape; stethoscope
  • Optional: OG/NG tube if ventilated and trained/allowed
  • Meds/equipment per local RSI/DSI pathway (if applicable) — follow your protocol

Steps (checklist)

1) Plan + assign roles (brief)

  • Primary plan: ETT
  • Backup 1: BVM (two-person) + suction
  • Backup 2: SGA
  • Backup 3: CICO (cricothyrotomy) if unable to oxygenate
  • Assign: laryngoscopist, BVM/oxygenation, suction, meds (if used), monitor/timekeeper

2) Pre-oxygenate / optimize physiology

  • Pre-oxygenate aggressively (BVM + PEEP if used locally; aim for best SpO2)
  • Position: "ear-to-sternal-notch" / ramp as needed; optimize external laryngeal position
  • Suction ("SALAD" mindset if soiled airway): keep suction in hand
  • Optimize hemodynamics when possible (fluids/pressors per protocol) before induction/PPV

3) Prepare device

  • Choose ETT size; check cuff; shape stylet ("hockey stick" gentle curve) or prepare bougie
  • Confirm laryngoscope functioning
  • Set ETCO2 ready to connect

4) Attempt (keep attempts controlled)

  • Laryngoscopy with continuous attention to oxygenation and time
  • Use suction early and often
  • If cords seen: pass tube through cords to appropriate depth (adult depth per local rule-of-thumb)
  • Inflate cuff; connect BVM/ventilator; begin ventilation
  • Limit attempt duration; if desaturation or difficulty:
    • Stop, re-oxygenate with BVM, reposition, change blade/device, use bougie, or move to SGA

5) Confirm placement (must-do)

  • Waveform capnography (ETCO2) confirmation
  • Bilateral chest rise + breath sounds; absence of gastric sounds
  • Reconfirm after securing and after any movement

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Persistent waveform ETCO2 with ventilation (preferred confirmation)
  • Improving oxygenation/ventilation and clinical status
  • Tube secured; stable depth marking; minimal leak

Complications + immediate management

  • Esophageal intubation
    • Recognize early (no ETCO2 waveform, poor chest rise) → remove tube, BVM, reattempt or SGA
  • Hypoxia/desaturation
    • Stop attempt, BVM + suction, reposition; consider SGA; escalate to CICO if cannot oxygenate
  • Aspiration/soiled airway
    • Aggressive suction; consider SGA/ETT strategy that restores oxygenation fastest
  • Hypotension/peri-intubation arrest
    • Treat shock, reduce ventilation pressures, consider push-dose/infusion per protocol; resuscitate per ACLS
  • Right mainstem intubation
    • Unequal breath sounds → withdraw slightly; reconfirm ETCO2 and bilateral ventilation
  • Dental/airway trauma
    • Gentle technique; optimize positioning and visualization

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Secure tube; document size, depth, confirmation method, number of attempts, complications
  • Continuous ETCO2 + SpO2; reassess after moves/transfer
  • Ventilation strategy: avoid hyperventilation; target appropriate ETCO2 per condition/protocol
  • Consider OG/NG decompression if appropriate and within local practice
