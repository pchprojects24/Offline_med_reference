Circulation / Hemorrhage Procedure

IO access

Indications

  • Emergent vascular access when IV access is delayed or unsuccessful
  • Cardiac arrest, shock, major trauma, severe dehydration, status epilepticus
  • Need for rapid medication/fluid delivery when time-critical

Contraindications / cautions

  • Fracture of the targeted bone or proximal fracture (extravasation/compartment risk)
  • Previous IO attempt in the same bone (recent)
  • Infection at insertion site
  • Compromised limb circulation or significant orthopedic hardware at site (use judgment)
  • Use site selection per your device/protocol

Equipment

  • IO device (manual or powered) + appropriate needle sizes
  • Antiseptic prep; sterile gloves as per protocol
  • Syringe with saline flush
  • Pressure bag or infusion pump (IO often needs pressure)
  • Extension tubing; securement
  • Analgesia plan for awake patient (IO infusion is painful) (VERIFY local meds/dose)

Common sites (adult — follow local protocol)

  • Proximal tibia
  • Proximal humerus
  • Distal tibia (alternative)
  • Site choice depends on training, device, and situation

Steps (checklist)

1) Prepare

  • Identify site and landmarks; expose and prep skin
  • Stabilize limb; confirm correct needle size

2) Insert

  • Insert per device technique until entry into medullary space is achieved (loss of resistance/"give")
  • Remove stylet; secure needle

3) Confirm and flush

  • Confirm placement:
    • Needle stands without support; aspiration may or may not yield marrow/blood
    • Easy flush without soft tissue swelling
  • Flush briskly with saline to clear the medullary space
  • Apply pressure infusion for fluids if needed

4) Infuse and monitor

  • Connect extension; secure well
  • If patient awake: manage pain for infusion per local protocol (VERIFY)
  • Monitor for extravasation and compartment syndrome

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Flushes without resistance beyond expected and without swelling
  • Meds/fluids flow with pressure
  • Clinical response to meds/fluids as expected

Complications + immediate management

  • Extravasation / compartment syndrome
    • Stop infusion; remove IO; elevate; urgent escalation
  • Misplacement (soft tissue)
    • Remove and reattempt at different site
  • Fracture (rare)
    • Immobilize, manage pain, escalate
  • Infection/osteomyelitis (rare in short-term use)
    • Limit dwell time; asepsis; remove when IV access obtained

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Use IO as a bridge: obtain IV access when feasible and remove IO per protocol timeframe
  • Reassess site frequently during infusion and after movement
  • Document: site, needle size, confirmation, meds/fluids given, complications
