Cardiac / Vascular

Metoprolol

Stocked on board

  • Metoprolol 25mg Tablets
  • Metoprolol 5mg/5mL Injection 5mL

Adult dosing

Symptomatic atrial fibrillation/flutter with rapid ventricular response (rate control; when appropriate)

  • IV: 5 mg slow IV push; may repeat every 5 minutes to a maximum of 15 mg (or earlier if target HR reached or hypotension develops)
  • PO (when stable/appropriate): 25–50 mg PO; repeat/titrate per response and local protocol

Suspected ACS / ischemic chest pain (selected patients; protocol-dependent)

  • IV: 5 mg slow IV push; may repeat every 5 minutes up to 15 mg if no contraindications
  • PO: 25–50 mg PO when stable/appropriate; ongoing dosing per local protocol/medical direction

Hypertension / angina (ongoing therapy; provider-directed)

  • Typical range: 25–50 mg PO once to twice daily; titrate per BP/HR and tolerance (local guidance)

Indications

  • Rate control for atrial fibrillation/flutter or other supraventricular tachyarrhythmias (protocol-dependent)
  • Adjunct therapy in ischemic heart disease/ACS in appropriate patients
  • Hypertension and angina (ongoing/chronic management)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Bradycardia, hypotension, or shock
  • 2nd/3rd degree AV block or sick sinus syndrome (unless paced)
  • Acute decompensated heart failure / cardiogenic shock
  • Severe bronchospasm/asthma (risk of bronchospasm; use caution)
  • Caution with diabetes (can mask hypoglycemia symptoms) and with other AV-nodal blockers (e.g., diltiazem/verapamil/digoxin/amiodarone) due to bradycardia/heart block risk

Adverse effects

  • Bradycardia, hypotension, dizziness/syncope
  • Worsening heart failure (fluid retention, dyspnea) in susceptible patients
  • Bronchospasm (susceptible patients)
  • Fatigue

Preparation & administration

IV formulation: 5 mg/5 mL (1 mg/mL)

  • Give slow IV push with continuous ECG/BP monitoring
  • Stop/escalate reassessment if significant hypotension, bradycardia, heart block, or bronchospasm occurs

PO tablets (25 mg)

  • Give with food when feasible; reassess HR/BP after dosing
  • Avoid rapid "stacking" of doses without reassessment

Monitoring

  • Continuous ECG/telemetry (when available), HR, BP, SpO2
  • Watch for bradycardia/AV block, hypotension, bronchospasm, and signs of heart failure
  • If diabetic: monitor glucose closely if clinically relevant

At-sea considerations

  • Use conservative dosing when monitoring capability is limited
  • Ensure readiness to manage adverse effects (fluids/vasopressors per protocol; pacing capability if bradycardia/heart block risk)
  • Document cumulative IV dose and response clearly to avoid dose stacking during handovers
