Metoclopramide

Stocked on board

  • Metoclopramide 5mg/mL Injection 2mL

Adult dosing

  • Nausea/vomiting (including migraine-associated nausea; protocol-dependent)
    • 0.1 mg/kg IV/IM/IO to a maximum single dose of 10 mg
    • May repeat every 6 hours as required
    • Total daily dose limit: 0.5 mg/kg/day (per protocol guidance)
  • Renal impairment
    • If renal impairment is known/suspected and ongoing dosing is needed: consider dose reduction and follow local guidance/medical direction

Indications

  • Symptomatic treatment of nausea and vomiting (including migraine-associated nausea)
  • Prokinetic effect may be useful when delayed gastric emptying is suspected (context dependent)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to metoclopramide
  • Suspected/known bowel obstruction or perforation, or GI bleeding (do not use)
  • Seizure disorder (may lower threshold)
  • Parkinsonism / history of extrapyramidal reactions or dystonia
  • MAOI use within the past 14 days (avoid)
  • Caution with QT-prolongation risk and in older adults (CNS effects/extrapyramidal risk)

Adverse effects

  • Akathisia/restlessness, anxiety
  • Acute dystonic reaction / other extrapyramidal symptoms (can be abrupt)
  • Drowsiness, fatigue
  • Diarrhea
  • Rare/serious: QT prolongation/arrhythmia risk (patient/context dependent); tardive dyskinesia with prolonged/repeated use

Preparation & administration

  • Supplied: 5 mg/mL in 2 mL vial (10 mg total)
  • IV/IM/IO: administer per local protocol; give IV doses slowly to reduce restlessness/adverse effects
  • If acute dystonia occurs: treat per local protocol (diphenhydramine is commonly used)

Monitoring

  • Symptom response (nausea/vomiting control; ability to hydrate/retain PO)
  • Observe for akathisia/dystonia/extrapyramidal symptoms after dosing
  • Level of alertness/sedation
  • If cardiac risk factors present: consider ECG/QT context per local practice

At-sea considerations

  • Have a plan for managing extrapyramidal reactions (recognize early; treat per protocol)
  • If patient needs to remain operational, consider sedation/akathisia impacts before dosing
  • Document dose/time carefully; avoid repeated dosing without reassessment
