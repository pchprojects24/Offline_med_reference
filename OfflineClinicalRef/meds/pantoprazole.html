Pantoprazole

Stocked on board

  • Pantoprazole 40mg Tablets
  • Pantoprazole 40mg Injection

Adult dosing

GERD / reflux symptoms (short-term)

  • 40 mg PO once daily

Erosive esophagitis / peptic ulcer disease (short-term)

  • 40 mg PO once daily (typical)

Unable to take PO or severe symptoms (protocol-dependent)

  • 40 mg IV once daily

Suspected upper GI bleed / high-risk ulcer disease (protocol-dependent)

  • IV dosing strategies vary; follow local protocol/medical direction

Indications

  • GERD and acid-related symptoms
  • Erosive esophagitis
  • Peptic ulcer disease (selected cases)
  • Acid suppression when PO not feasible (IV use; protocol-dependent)
  • Part of selected upper GI bleed pathways (protocol-dependent)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to pantoprazole/PPIs
  • Concomitant use with rilpivirine (avoid)
  • Caution: long-term/high-dose use (if prolonged), history of hypomagnesemia, severe liver disease
  • Interaction considerations: clopidogrel (clinical significance varies; use local guidance)

Adverse effects

  • Headache, diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain
  • Long-term use (if prolonged): hypomagnesemia, B12 deficiency risk, fractures/infections (context-dependent)

Preparation & administration

PO tablets

  • Swallow whole; do not crush/chew if delayed-release formulation

IV injection

  • Reconstitute/dilute and administer per product monograph/local protocol
  • Use appropriate IV compatibility and flush practices per local guidance

Monitoring

  • Clinical response (pain, reflux symptoms, bleeding symptoms if relevant)
  • If prolonged therapy or high-risk patient: magnesium and B12 when feasible; assess for diarrhea/infection risk
  • If GI bleed suspected: hemodynamics and ongoing bleeding signs per broader pathway

At-sea considerations

  • If diagnostics are limited, treat symptomatically but reassess for red flags (GI bleed, persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain)
  • Document start date/indication to avoid unnecessary prolonged PPI use onboard
  • If clopidogrel is being used for ACS pathways, apply local guidance regarding co-administration
