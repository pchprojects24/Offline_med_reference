Dexamethasone

Stocked on board

  • Dexamethasone 4mg Tablets
  • Dexamethasone 4mg/mL Injection

Adult dosing

Severe allergic reaction / anaphylaxis adjunct (does not replace epinephrine)

  • 4–10 mg IV/IM (or PO if able) as adjunct therapy; follow local protocol/medical direction

Acute asthma/COPD exacerbation (systemic steroid; protocol-dependent)

  • 8–16 mg PO/IV once, then 4–8 mg daily for a short course (use local guidance)

Severe pharyngitis/airway inflammation (selected cases; protocol-dependent)

  • Single dose 8–10 mg PO/IM/IV (local guidance)

Indications

  • Anti-inflammatory / immunosuppressive steroid for selected acute conditions (protocol-dependent)
  • Adjunct in severe allergic reactions (not first-line)
  • Acute exacerbations of obstructive airway disease when systemic steroid indicated
  • Other specialist-directed indications (e.g., cerebral edema) — follow medical direction

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity
  • Untreated systemic infection (relative; risk–benefit in time-critical scenarios)
  • Caution in: diabetes (hyperglycemia), hypertension, peptic ulcer disease/GI bleed risk, psychiatric history (mood/psychosis), glaucoma
  • Avoid routine use without a clear indication; use shortest effective course

Adverse effects

  • Hyperglycemia
  • Mood changes, agitation, insomnia
  • GI upset; increased infection risk (dose/course dependent)
  • Fluid retention, hypertension (dose dependent)

Preparation & administration

PO tablets

  • Give with food when feasible to reduce GI upset

Injection (4 mg/mL)

  • Administer IV/IM per product monograph/local protocol
  • Use appropriate dilution/administration rate per local guidance

Use the lowest effective dose and reassess need for repeat dosing

Monitoring

  • Clinical response (airway symptoms, allergic symptoms)
  • Glucose monitoring when feasible in diabetics/high-risk patients
  • Mental status/mood changes, BP if clinically relevant
  • Infection signs if prolonged/repeated dosing

At-sea considerations

  • If labs are limited, be conservative with repeat dosing and document indication/dose/time clearly
  • In diabetics, anticipate hyperglycemia; plan monitoring and follow onboard diabetes protocols
  • For allergic reactions, emphasize that dexamethasone is adjunctive—ensure core therapy and observation are addressed
