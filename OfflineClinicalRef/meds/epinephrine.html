Cardiac / Vascular

Epinephrine

Stocked on board

  • Epinephrine 1mg/mL Injection 1mL Ampoules

Adult dosing

Cardiac arrest (ACLS)

  • 1 mg IV/IO every 3–5 minutes

Anaphylaxis (adult)

  • IM 0.5 mg (0.5 mL of 1 mg/mL) into mid-anterolateral thigh; repeat every 5–15 minutes as needed
  • If refractory with impending arrest/shock: IV epinephrine should be reserved for expert use with close monitoring (local protocol/medical direction)

Severe bradycardia / hypotension (when escalation needed)

  • Infusion dosing varies by protocol; titrate to effect if used (local protocol/medical direction)

Indications

  • Anaphylaxis and severe allergic reaction with systemic features
  • Cardiac arrest per ACLS
  • Severe bronchospasm with impending respiratory failure (as per protocol)
  • Critical hypotension/bradycardia when other measures insufficient (protocol-dependent)

Contraindications / cautions

  • In life-threatening anaphylaxis or cardiac arrest: no practical absolute contraindications
  • High-risk cautions (especially IV use): ischemic heart disease, significant hypertension, dysrhythmia risk, hyperthyroidism, diabetes, elderly
  • Avoid dosing/concentration errors: 1 mg/mL (1:1000) vs 0.1 mg/mL (1:10,000) preparations may both exist in some systems

Preparation & administration

  • Protect from light; do not use if solution is pink/darker than slightly yellow or has precipitate
  • IM: mid-anterolateral thigh preferred for absorption (when treating anaphylaxis)
  • IV/IO: use only with continuous monitoring and clear indication (ACLS/shock protocols)

Monitoring

  • Continuous ECG, BP, SpO2; reassess airway/ventilation frequently
  • Watch for tachyarrhythmias, severe hypertension, chest pain/ischemia, tremor/anxiety

Adverse effects

  • Tachycardia, palpitations, hypertension
  • Tremor, anxiety, headache
  • Dysrhythmias, myocardial ischemia (risk increases with IV bolus/dose errors)

At-sea considerations

  • Label syringes clearly ("EPI 1 mg/mL") to prevent concentration errors
  • Prefer IM for anaphylaxis early; reserve IV for impending arrest/shock under protocol
  • Use checklists/closed-loop communication during ACLS to avoid timing/dose drift
