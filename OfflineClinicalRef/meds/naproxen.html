Analgesia / Anti-inflammatory

Naproxen

Stocked on board

  • Naproxen 250mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Acute musculoskeletal pain / inflammatory pain

  • 250–500 mg PO twice daily as needed (use 1–2 x 250 mg tablets per dose)
  • Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration

Dysmenorrhea (if relevant onboard)

  • 500 mg PO once, then 250 mg PO every 6–8 hours as needed (max daily limits per local guidance)

Indications

  • Mild–moderate pain (especially inflammatory/musculoskeletal)
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Acute gout or other inflammatory flares (protocol/diagnosis dependent)

Contraindications / cautions

Avoid/contraindicated:

  • NSAID hypersensitivity (incl. ASA/NSAID-exacerbated respiratory disease)
  • Active GI bleeding or active peptic ulcer disease
  • Severe renal impairment or significant acute kidney injury
  • Peri-operative CABG setting
  • Pregnancy: avoid especially in 3rd trimester (ductal/renal fetal risks)

Use caution in:

  • Older adults, dehydration, CKD risk, heart failure, uncontrolled HTN
  • Concurrent anticoagulants/antiplatelets, systemic steroids, heavy alcohol use (bleeding risk)
  • History of GI ulcer/bleed (risk–benefit; consider local gastroprotection practice)

Adverse effects

  • Dyspepsia, nausea
  • GI ulceration/bleeding (can occur without warning)
  • Fluid retention, ↑BP; can worsen heart failure
  • Renal function deterioration (higher risk with dehydration/CKD)
  • Hypersensitivity/bronchospasm in susceptible patients

Preparation & administration

  • Take with food if GI upset occurs
  • Avoid combining with other NSAIDs (ibuprofen/ketorolac/etc.)
  • If patient is on low-dose ASA for cardiac indications: NSAIDs may increase bleeding risk; document and use caution

Monitoring

  • Pain relief and function
  • GI symptoms (melena/hematemesis, persistent epigastric pain)
  • BP/edema
  • Renal function if prolonged use or higher-risk patient (where feasible)

At-sea considerations

  • Higher risk in dehydration, limited labs, and limited evac options: be conservative with duration/dose
  • Document start date/time and reassess frequently; stop if GI bleed symptoms or significant BP/edema occur
  • Avoid stacking NSAIDs when multiple prescribers are involved (clear med reconciliation onboard)
