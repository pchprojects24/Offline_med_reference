Respiratory / Allergy

Cetirizine

Stocked on board

  • Cetirizine 10mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Allergic rhinitis (seasonal/perennial)
    • 10 mg PO once daily as needed
  • Urticaria (hives) / pruritus from allergic triggers
    • 10 mg PO once daily
  • If sensitive to sedation
    • Consider 5 mg PO once daily (half-tablet) if feasible/appropriate; reassess

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of allergic rhinitis (sneezing, rhinorrhea, itchy/watery eyes)
  • Symptomatic relief of urticaria and itch related to allergic triggers

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to cetirizine, hydroxyzine, or piperazine derivatives
  • Sedation can occur (less than first-generation antihistamines but still possible): caution with watchkeeping, machinery, and alcohol/sedatives
  • Renal impairment: dose reduction may be required (follow product monograph/local guidance)
  • Caution in older adults if fall risk or concurrent sedatives

Adverse effects

  • Drowsiness/fatigue, dizziness
  • Dry mouth
  • Headache
  • GI upset (uncommon)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; may take with or without food
  • If drowsy, consider taking in the evening (operationally) and reassess daytime performance
  • Avoid combining with other sedating meds/alcohol when possible

Monitoring

  • Symptom control (rhinitis/urticaria)
  • Sedation/functional impairment (especially first dose and when combined with other CNS depressants)
  • If prolonged use in renal impairment: monitor for increased sedation and reassess need/dose

At-sea considerations

  • Operational safety: warn about drowsiness; consider first dose at a time when the member is not on critical duties
  • If symptoms are primarily nasal congestion, ensure non-sedating options and local pathways are also considered (e.g., nasal steroid use if stocked and appropriate)
  • Document response and any sedation to guide future selection (cetirizine vs loratadine, etc.)
