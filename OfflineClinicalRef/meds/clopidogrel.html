Cardiac / Vascular

Clopidogrel

Stocked on board

  • Clopidogrel 75mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Acute coronary syndrome (ACS) / unstable angina / NSTEMI (typically with ASA)

  • Loading: 300 mg PO once (4 x 75 mg tablets) when a loading dose is indicated
  • Maintenance: 75 mg PO once daily

STEMI with suspected thrombolysis pathway (protocol-dependent; often prehospital/remote)

  • If < 75 years: 300 mg PO once, then 75 mg PO daily
  • If ≥ 75 years: 75 mg PO once (no loading), then 75 mg PO daily

Secondary prevention (post-MI, ischemic stroke/TIA, peripheral arterial disease) when clopidogrel is selected

  • 75 mg PO once daily

Indications

  • Antiplatelet therapy for ACS (often as dual antiplatelet therapy with ASA) when directed by protocol/medical direction
  • Secondary prevention of atherothrombotic events (post-MI, ischemic stroke, PAD) when clopidogrel is selected
  • When ASA is contraindicated/intolerant and antiplatelet therapy is still indicated (provider-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

Contraindications

  • Active pathological bleeding (e.g., GI bleed, intracranial hemorrhage)
  • Hypersensitivity to clopidogrel
  • Significant hepatic impairment/cholestatic jaundice

Cautions

  • High bleeding risk (recent surgery/trauma, peptic ulcer disease, concomitant anticoagulants)
  • Drug interactions that reduce effect (CYP2C19 inhibitors) or increase bleeding risk
  • Consider reduced response in CYP2C19 poor metabolizers (if known)

Adverse effects

  • Bleeding/bruising (most common; can be occult)
  • GI upset/diarrhea, rash (less common)
  • Rare but serious: thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura (TTP) or severe thrombocytopenia (seek urgent care if suspected)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablets: administer with or without food
  • If a loading dose is needed and only 75 mg tablets are stocked: 300 mg load = 4 tablets once
  • Avoid "double dosing" if the patient already took clopidogrel—confirm history if possible

Monitoring

  • Watch for bleeding: melena, hematemesis, hematuria, easy bruising, hypotension without obvious source
  • Consider baseline CBC if starting ongoing therapy; reassess if bleeding/bruising develops
  • If severe headache/neuro change: consider intracranial bleed (evac/escalate)

At-sea considerations

  • High consequence of bleeding in remote settings: document dose/time clearly and reassess frequently
  • If evacuation/operative care may be needed soon, communicate recent clopidogrel use early
  • If a PPI is required for GI protection, avoid strong CYP2C19-inhibiting PPIs when possible; pantoprazole is often used when a PPI is needed (follow local guidance)
