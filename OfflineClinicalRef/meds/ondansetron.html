Ondansetron

Stocked on board

  • Ondansetron 4mg Tablets
  • Ondansetron 2mg/mL Injection 2mL

Adult dosing

Nausea/vomiting (general, adult)

  • PO: 4 mg once; consider repeat dosing only if allowed by your local protocol/medical direction
  • IV/IM/SC: 4 mg (common); administer IV slowly (e.g., over ~2â€“5 minutes) to reduce adverse effects

Post-op / medication-related nausea

  • Same approach: 4 mg PO or 4 mg parenteral commonly used; tailor to clinical context

Indications

  • Nausea and vomiting (multiple causes; use when clinically appropriate)
  • Prevention/treatment of significant nausea that interferes with hydration/PO meds

Contraindications / cautions

  • Concomitant apomorphine is contraindicated (severe hypotension/LOC risk)
  • QT prolongation risk: caution with known long QT, significant electrolyte abnormalities, or other QT-prolonging drugs
  • Severe hepatic impairment: dosing limits may apply (protocol/monograph dependent)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet: swallow with water
  • IV: give slowly; ensure line patency; flush per local practice
  • Avoid rapid IV push if protocol advises slower administration

Monitoring

  • Symptom response (nausea/vomiting frequency), hydration status
  • If risk factors: consider ECG monitoring and address electrolytes when possible

Adverse effects

  • Headache
  • Constipation
  • QT prolongation (rare but important), dizziness

At-sea considerations

  • Useful "function-restoring" med: enables oral fluids/meds and reduces dehydration risk at sea
  • If severe ongoing vomiting: reassess for surgical/critical causes and plan escalation/evac early
  • Consider motion-related triggers; pair with non-pharm measures when appropriate
