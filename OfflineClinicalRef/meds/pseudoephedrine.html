Pseudoephedrine

Stocked on board

  • Pseudoephedrine 60mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Nasal/sinus congestion (adult)

  • 60 mg PO every 4–6 hours as needed
  • Max 240 mg/day
  • Use the shortest duration needed; reassess if not improving after several days

Indications

  • Temporary relief of nasal congestion and sinus congestion/pressure due to common cold or allergic rhinitis (symptomatic)

Contraindications / cautions

Avoid/contraindicated:

  • Use within 14 days of MAOI use
  • Severe/uncontrolled hypertension or significant cardiovascular disease (risk of BP/HR increase)
  • Significant arrhythmias
  • Hyperthyroidism (sympathomimetic sensitivity)
  • Narrow-angle glaucoma
  • Urinary retention / severe BPH (may worsen retention)

Use caution in: older adults, insomnia/anxiety, patients sensitive to stimulants, and when combined with other sympathomimetics/caffeine

Adverse effects

  • Insomnia, nervousness/jitteriness, tremor
  • Palpitations, tachycardia, increased blood pressure
  • Anxiety, headache
  • Urinary retention (susceptible patients)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; avoid late-day dosing if insomnia is an issue
  • Avoid combining with other decongestants/stimulants unless specifically intended

Monitoring

  • Symptom response (congestion/pressure)
  • BP/HR in higher-risk patients or if symptoms of palpitations/chest discomfort occur
  • Watch for urinary retention (difficulty voiding)

At-sea considerations

  • Sleep and watchkeeping: can cause insomnia/jitteriness—dose timing matters
  • If limited ability to monitor BP, be conservative in members with known HTN/CV risk
  • If severe headache with sudden onset, visual changes, confusion, seizures, or neurologic symptoms occur: stop drug and escalate care
