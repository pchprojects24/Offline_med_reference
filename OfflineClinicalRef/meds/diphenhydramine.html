Respiratory / Allergy

Diphenhydramine

Stocked on board

  • Diphenhydramine 25mg Capsules
  • Diphenhydramine 50mg/mL Injection 1mL

Adult dosing

Allergic reaction / urticaria (symptom relief; NOT first-line for life-threatening anaphylaxis)

  • PO: 25–50 mg every 4–6 hours as needed
  • IM/IV: 25–50 mg as a single dose (typical), may repeat per protocol/medical direction

Adjunct for anaphylaxis (itching/urticaria only; AFTER epinephrine when indicated)

  • IM/IV: 25–50 mg (common)

Motion sickness / vestibular nausea (if used)

  • PO: 25–50 mg every 4–6 hours as needed (sedating)

Indications

  • Allergic symptoms (urticaria, pruritus, mild allergic reactions)
  • Adjunct symptom control in anaphylaxis (does not treat airway/shock)
  • Motion sickness / vertigo-associated nausea (sedating option)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to diphenhydramine/antihistamines
  • Significant sedation/impairment: avoid driving/operating machinery
  • Anticholinergic cautions: narrow-angle glaucoma, urinary retention/BPH, severe constipation/ileus risk
  • Additive CNS depression with alcohol, opioids, benzodiazepines, other sedatives
  • IV administration: avoid rapid IV push; hypotension and excessive sedation risk (follow local protocol)

Preparation & administration

  • PO: take with water; onset may be slower than parenteral routes
  • IM/IV: give per protocol; monitor closely for sedation/hypotension
  • Avoid dosing errors in frail/older adults (delirium/oversedation risk)

Monitoring

  • Mental status/sedation, respiratory status (if co-sedatives used)
  • BP/HR (especially if IV)
  • Symptom response (itching/hives), progression of allergic reaction

Adverse effects

  • Drowsiness/sedation, dizziness
  • Dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention (anticholinergic)
  • Hypotension (more likely with IV/rapid administration)
  • Paradoxical agitation (uncommon)

At-sea considerations

  • Do not let antihistamine use delay epinephrine in true anaphylaxis
  • Sedation can impair watchkeeping/workplace safety; document counsel
  • Consider less-sedating alternatives for routine allergy symptoms when appropriate (e.g., cetirizine/loratadine)
