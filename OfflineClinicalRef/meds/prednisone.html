Prednisone

Stocked on board

  • Prednisone 50mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Acute asthma exacerbation (adult)
    • 50 mg PO once daily for at least 5 days (course length per protocol/clinical response)
  • Acute COPD exacerbation (adult)
    • 40 mg PO once daily for 5 days (if only 50 mg tablets are available onboard, 50 mg PO daily is often used in practice; use local guidance)
  • Severe allergic reaction (adjunct; not first-line for anaphylaxis)
    • 50 mg PO once daily for a short course (e.g., 3–5 days) if used; reassess

Indications

  • Acute asthma exacerbation
  • Acute COPD exacerbation
  • Other inflammatory/allergic conditions where a short course systemic corticosteroid is appropriate (provider-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to prednisone or formulation components
  • Active, untreated serious infection: steroids may worsen/obscure infection (use risk–benefit and reassess frequently)
  • Caution (common onboard relevance):
    • Diabetes (hyperglycemia)
    • Psychiatric history (mood changes, agitation, insomnia)
    • Hypertension/heart failure (fluid retention)
    • Peptic ulcer/GI bleeding risk (especially with NSAIDs/ASA)
    • Immunosuppressed states / recent live vaccines (provider-directed)

Adverse effects

  • Hyperglycemia
  • Mood changes (irritability/euphoria), insomnia
  • GI upset/dyspepsia
  • Fluid retention, increased appetite
  • Increased infection risk (dose/duration dependent)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; take with food if possible to reduce GI upset
  • Prefer morning dosing to reduce insomnia (operationally)
  • Short "burst" courses usually do NOT require tapering; tapering may be needed if prolonged use or chronic steroid exposure (use local guidance)
  • Avoid abrupt discontinuation in patients on chronic steroids (adrenal suppression risk)

Monitoring

  • Symptom response (respiratory status, peak flow if used locally, work of breathing)
  • Blood glucose in diabetics or those at risk
  • Mood/sleep disturbance
  • Signs of infection or clinical deterioration
  • GI symptoms/bleeding risk if combined with NSAIDs/ASA

At-sea considerations

  • Stock is 50 mg tablets: plan dosing around this strength and document dose/time clearly to avoid "partial tab" confusion
  • If infection is plausible (e.g., pneumonia), reassess frequently and avoid anchoring on "inflammation-only"
  • Consider GI protection strategy and bleeding risk if concurrent NSAIDs/ASA are being used
  • Document start date, planned duration, and stop date to prevent unintended prolonged courses
