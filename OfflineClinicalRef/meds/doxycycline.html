Doxycycline

Stocked on board

  • Doxycycline 100mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Chlamydia (uncomplicated, non-pregnant adults)
    • 100 mg PO twice daily for 7 days
  • Skin/soft tissue infection (selected cases; follow local guidance for organism risk)
    • 100 mg PO twice daily (typical duration 5–10 days depending on severity/response)
  • Community-acquired pneumonia (selected outpatient cases; follow local guidance)
    • 100 mg PO twice daily (typical duration ~5–7 days depending on severity/response)

Indications

  • Susceptible bacterial infections where doxycycline is appropriate (respiratory, skin/soft tissue, some STIs)
  • Chlamydia treatment (first-line in non-pregnant adults)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Contraindicated in tetracycline hypersensitivity
  • Avoid in pregnancy (especially 2nd/3rd trimester) and generally avoid during breastfeeding unless directed (tooth/bone effects)
  • Avoid in children <8 years unless specialist-directed (tooth discoloration/bone effects)
  • Esophagitis/esophageal ulcer risk: take with plenty of water; remain upright after dose
  • Photosensitivity risk (sunburn): counsel on sun protection
  • Drug/food interactions that reduce absorption:
    • Separate from iron, calcium, magnesium, aluminum antacids, bismuth, zinc, and multivitamins (separate by at least 2–3 hours)
  • Interaction cautions: warfarin (INR effects), isotretinoin (intracranial hypertension risk)

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea), pill esophagitis
  • Photosensitivity rash/sunburn
  • Vaginal/oral candidiasis
  • Rare/serious: hepatotoxicity, severe rash, intracranial hypertension

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablets: give with a full glass of water
  • Take sitting/standing; avoid lying down for at least 30 minutes after dose
  • May take with food to reduce GI upset (avoid taking with dairy/antacids/minerals at the same time)
  • Separate from iron/calcium/magnesium/aluminum/zinc products by at least 2–3 hours

Monitoring

  • Clinical response (fever/pain/lesion improvement) within expected timeframe
  • GI tolerance; counsel to report severe/persistent diarrhea
  • If prolonged therapy or significant comorbidity: consider LFTs per clinician judgment
  • If on warfarin: consider INR monitoring per clinician judgment

At-sea considerations

  • Sun exposure at sea can be significant: reinforce sun protection and photosensitivity precautions
  • Simplify dosing schedule (BID) and document start/stop dates clearly to support adherence
  • Flag "separate from minerals/antacids" because shipboard patients often take supplements
  • If pregnancy is possible: screen before use and preferentially use alternatives where indicated
