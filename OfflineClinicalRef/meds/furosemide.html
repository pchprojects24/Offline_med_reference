Cardiac / Vascular

Furosemide

Stocked on board

  • Furosemide 20mg Tablets
  • Furosemide 10mg/mL Injection 2mL

Adult dosing

Acute pulmonary edema / acute heart failure with volume overload (protocol-dependent)

  • IV: 20–40 mg IV; reassess response (BP, work of breathing, urine output) and repeat/escalate per local protocol

Peripheral edema/volume overload (non-emergent)

  • PO: 20–40 mg PO; titrate per clinical response and local guidance

Adjunct in hyperkalemia (if producing urine; protocol-dependent)

  • IV or PO dosing per local protocol as part of a broader hyperkalemia pathway (not as monotherapy)

Indications

  • Volume overload/edema (e.g., heart failure) and pulmonary edema (protocol-dependent)
  • Adjunct diuresis in selected scenarios where fluid removal is desired (provider/protocol-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Anuria or severe dehydration/hypovolemia
  • Hypotension/shock (may worsen)
  • Significant electrolyte depletion (especially hypokalemia, hyponatremia, hypomagnesemia)
  • Renal dysfunction: use caution; monitor response and renal function when possible
  • Ototoxicity risk with high doses/rapid IV administration (dose/rate dependent)

Adverse effects

  • Hypotension, dizziness, dehydration
  • Electrolyte abnormalities (↓K, ↓Na, ↓Mg) and metabolic alkalosis
  • Worsening renal function (pre-renal azotemia) if over-diuresed
  • Ototoxicity (rare; higher risk with rapid/high-dose IV)

Preparation & administration

IV formulation: 10 mg/mL; stocked 2 mL = 20 mg total

  • Administer IV slowly and reassess frequently (BP, symptoms, urine output)

PO tablets: 20 mg

  • Ensure access to fluids as clinically appropriate; avoid over-diuresis

Consider potassium strategy per local protocol if ongoing diuresis planned and K is available onboard

Monitoring

  • BP, HR, SpO2; clinical work of breathing (if pulmonary edema)
  • Urine output and hydration status
  • Electrolytes and renal function when available (K/Na/Mg/creatinine)
  • Daily weights/fluid balance if prolonged therapy

At-sea considerations

  • Lab limitations: dose conservatively and rely on clinical reassessment (BP, orthostasis, urine output, symptoms)
  • Avoid over-diuresis in heat/exertion/limited water intake environments
  • Document dose/time and response; track cumulative dosing during prolonged cases
