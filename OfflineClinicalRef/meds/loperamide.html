Loperamide

Stocked on board

  • Loperamide 2mg Capsules

Adult dosing

  • Acute, non-bloody diarrhea (adjunct to oral rehydration)
    • 4 mg PO once (2 capsules), then 2 mg PO (1 capsule) after each loose stool
    • Max: 8 mg/day (4 capsules) unless directed otherwise by medical authority
    • Stop and reassess if symptoms persist >48 hours, or if red flags develop
  • Chronic diarrhea (provider-directed)
    • Use lowest effective dose; reassess cause and ongoing need

Indications

  • Symptomatic control of acute, non-specific non-bloody diarrhea (as an adjunct to rehydration)
  • Selected chronic diarrhea situations (provider-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Suspected infectious dysentery: blood/mucus in stool and/or high fever
  • Suspected or confirmed C. difficile / antibiotic-associated colitis (avoid antimotility agents)
  • Acute ulcerative colitis flare or toxic megacolon risk (avoid)
  • Significant abdominal distension, ileus, or suspected obstruction (avoid)
  • Caution:
    • Hepatic impairment (increased CNS effects)
    • Overuse/high doses can cause serious cardiac arrhythmias (do not exceed recommended dose)

Adverse effects

  • Constipation
  • Abdominal cramps, nausea
  • Dizziness/drowsiness (uncommon)
  • Rare but serious (usually overdose/misuse): ileus, toxic megacolon, QT prolongation/arrhythmias

Preparation & administration

  • PO capsules with water
  • Prioritize rehydration (oral rehydration solution if available); loperamide is symptom control, not definitive treatment
  • Avoid combining with other constipating agents unless specifically intended and monitored

Monitoring

  • Stool frequency/consistency and abdominal pain
  • Hydration status (orthostasis, urine output, mucous membranes)
  • Red flags: fever, bloody stool, severe pain, dehydration, worsening distension, persistent symptoms >48 hours
  • If constipation develops, stop and reassess

At-sea considerations

  • In outbreaks (shipboard gastroenteritis): focus on hydration, isolation/hygiene measures, and careful screening for red flags
  • Avoid masking severe infectious illness; low threshold to reassess if systemic symptoms, dehydration, or blood/fever present
  • Document dose/timing to prevent accidental overuse; ensure maximum daily dose is not exceeded
