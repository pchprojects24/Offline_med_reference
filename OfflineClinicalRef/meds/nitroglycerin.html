Nitroglycerin

Stocked on board

  • Nitroglycerin 0.4mg Spray
  • Nitroglycerin 0.2mg/hr Patch

Adult dosing

Suspected angina / ischemic chest pain (acute relief â€” SPRAY)

  • 0.4 mg (1 spray) SL onto/under tongue
  • Repeat every 5 minutes as needed up to a typical max of 3 doses total
  • Sit/lie down before dosing (hypotension/syncope risk)

Chronic angina prophylaxis (PATCH)

  • Patch dosing/usage is protocol-directed; not for immediate relief of acute chest pain

Indications

  • Acute relief of angina-type chest pain (spray)
  • Angina prophylaxis (patch; maintenance use)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypotension or shock; marked bradycardia/tachycardia (use clinical judgment)
  • Suspected right ventricular infarct / inferior STEMI with preload dependence (caution)
  • Recent PDE-5 inhibitors (e.g., sildenafil/tadalafil/vardenafil) due to profound hypotension risk
  • Severe aortic stenosis / hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy (caution)
  • Head injury / raised ICP concerns (caution; headache/vasodilation)

Preparation & administration

  • Spray: deliver onto/under tongue; do not inhale during actuation; close mouth after dosing
  • Avoid eating/drinking/smoking immediately around dosing (sublingual absorption)
  • If dizziness occurs: keep patient supine and reassess BP

Monitoring

  • BP and symptoms before each dose; reassess pain response after each dose
  • Watch for hypotension, syncope, reflex tachycardia, worsening symptoms

Adverse effects

  • Headache (very common)
  • Hypotension, dizziness, syncope
  • Flushing, tachycardia

At-sea considerations

  • Treat as a "reassess every dose" medication: BP + symptom check before repeating
  • If pain persists after typical max dosing or patient deteriorates: escalate/evac plan early
  • Patch is not an acute chest-pain tool; keep acute pathway focused on spray + ACS workup
