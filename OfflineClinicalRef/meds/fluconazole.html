Fluconazole

Stocked on board

  • Fluconazole 150mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Vulvovaginal candidiasis (uncomplicated)
    • 150 mg PO once (single dose)
  • Severe vulvovaginal candidiasis (if significant symptoms; follow local guidance)
    • 150 mg PO, then repeat 150 mg PO 72 hours later (some cases may require an additional dose per guidance/clinical judgment)
  • Candidal balanitis (selected cases; if clinician-directed)
    • 150 mg PO once

Indications

  • Vulvovaginal candidiasis (single-dose oral treatment)
  • Selected mucocutaneous Candida infections where oral fluconazole is appropriate (clinician-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Contraindicated in known fluconazole/azole hypersensitivity
  • Pregnancy: avoid oral fluconazole for vaginal candidiasis in pregnancy when possible; prefer topical therapy (risk-benefit)
  • Hepatic risk: caution in significant liver disease; hepatotoxicity is rare but possible
  • QT prolongation risk: caution with known long QT, electrolyte abnormalities, or other QT-prolonging drugs
  • Major drug interaction potential (CYP inhibition):
    • Warfarin (↑ INR/bleeding risk)
    • Some sulfonylureas (hypoglycemia risk)
    • Some statins (myopathy risk) and other CYP-metabolized agents
    • Review med list when feasible, especially if repeat dosing planned

Adverse effects

  • Headache, nausea, abdominal discomfort/diarrhea
  • Rash (rare severe reactions)
  • Rare/serious: hepatotoxicity, QT prolongation/arrhythmia

Preparation & administration

  • Single 150 mg PO dose: with or without food
  • Counsel: symptom improvement may take 24–72 hours; persistent symptoms need reassessment (diagnosis/complicated infection)
  • If repeat dosing is used: document each dose date/time clearly and reassess symptoms

Monitoring

  • For single-dose use: usually symptom-based monitoring only
  • If repeat/prolonged therapy: consider LFTs and interaction monitoring per clinician judgment
  • If on warfarin: consider INR monitoring if feasible

At-sea considerations

  • Screen for pregnancy when relevant; topical alternatives may be preferred in pregnancy
  • Keep a simple "single-dose given" note with date/time to avoid duplicate dosing
  • If symptoms persist or recur, consider limited diagnostics at sea and plan follow-up/evac guidance based on severity and risk factors
  • Interaction risk: if the patient is on multiple meds, do a quick interaction check before repeat dosing if feasible
