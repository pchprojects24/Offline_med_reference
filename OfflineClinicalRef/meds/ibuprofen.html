Analgesia / Anti-inflammatory

Ibuprofen

Stocked on board

  • Ibuprofen 400mg Tablets
  • Ibuprofen 600mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Pain/fever (typical short-term use)
    • 400 mg PO every 6–8 hours as needed
    • If needing higher single doses and appropriate for patient risk: 600 mg PO every 8 hours as needed
    • Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration
  • Anti-inflammatory dosing (selected cases)
    • 600 mg PO every 8 hours short-term when needed (reassess frequently)
  • Maximum daily dose (practical onboard guidance)
    • Avoid high-dose regimens in higher-risk patients; do not exceed 2400 mg/day without clear indication and careful risk assessment

Indications

  • Mild to moderate pain (MSK pain, dental pain, headache)
  • Fever
  • Inflammatory pain (sprains/strains; selected arthritis flares)

Contraindications / cautions

  • NSAID hypersensitivity (including ASA-exacerbated respiratory disease)
  • Active GI bleeding or active peptic ulcer disease
  • Significant renal impairment/dehydration or deteriorating renal function
  • Severe heart failure or significant cardiovascular disease/risk (higher doses increase risk)
  • Pregnancy: avoid in 3rd trimester; use caution/avoid from ~20 weeks onward per warnings; follow local guidance
  • Concomitant anticoagulants/antiplatelets, heavy alcohol use, older age → higher bleeding risk

Adverse effects

  • Dyspepsia, nausea
  • GI ulceration/bleeding
  • Fluid retention, ↑BP; renal function decline (especially with dehydration/ACEi/diuretics)
  • Cardiovascular thrombotic events risk increases with higher dose/duration

Preparation & administration

  • Take with food or milk if GI upset
  • Avoid stacking with other NSAIDs (naproxen, ketorolac, etc.)
  • If patient is on low-dose ASA for CV prevention, coordinate timing per local guidance (ibuprofen can interfere with ASA antiplatelet effect)

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief and ongoing need (stop when no longer needed)
  • GI symptoms (melena, hematemesis, severe epigastric pain)
  • BP/edema (if at risk)
  • Renal risk: ensure hydration; consider renal function monitoring when feasible in higher-risk patients

At-sea considerations

  • Dehydration/heat stress increases renal/GI risk—prioritize hydration and limit NSAID exposure
  • If limited labs, be conservative in older patients, those with renal disease, or on ACEi/ARB/diuretics
  • Document dose/time carefully to avoid "double-dosing" with other NSAIDs onboard
