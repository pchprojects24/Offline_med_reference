Respiratory / Allergy

Salbutamol

Stocked on board

  • Salbutamol 100mcg Inhaler

Adult dosing

Acute bronchospasm (asthma/COPD flare)

  • MDI: 1–2 puffs (100 mcg per puff) every 4–6 hours as needed
  • For significant acute symptoms: higher "burst" dosing may be used per protocol (e.g., multiple puffs via spacer), then reassess

Prevention of exercise-induced bronchospasm

  • 1–2 puffs 15 minutes before exertion (as needed)

Anaphylaxis with wheeze (adjunct only)

  • May help bronchospasm AFTER epinephrine has been given when indicated; not a replacement for epinephrine

Indications

  • Relief and prevention of bronchospasm in asthma
  • Symptomatic relief of bronchospasm in COPD
  • Adjunct bronchodilation in allergic reactions/anaphylaxis with wheeze (after epinephrine when indicated)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to salbutamol or inhaler components (rare)
  • Caution with significant tachyarrhythmias, ischemic heart disease, uncontrolled hyperthyroidism
  • Can cause hypokalemia (more likely with high doses, concomitant diuretics, severe illness)
  • Overuse without reassessment may mask deterioration; reassess work of breathing and oxygenation

Preparation & administration

  • Shake inhaler; use correct technique
  • Spacer strongly preferred when available (improves delivery, reduces oropharyngeal deposition)
  • Typical technique: Exhale → seal lips → actuate once at start of slow deep breath → hold 10 seconds → wait ~30–60 sec between puffs
  • If patient is too breathless to coordinate: use spacer with tidal breathing

Monitoring

  • Work of breathing, SpO2, ability to speak, peak flow if available
  • HR and rhythm (tachycardia, tremor)
  • Reassess after initial doses before repeating high-dose cycles

Adverse effects

  • Tremor, anxiety, headache
  • Tachycardia/palpitations
  • Transient worsening cough/irritation
  • Hypokalemia (dose-related)

At-sea considerations

  • Ensure good inhaler technique; many "failures" are technique-related—use spacer when possible
  • If needing frequent repeat dosing or symptoms recur quickly, escalate care/evac planning early
  • Separate "bronchospasm relief" from "underlying cause" (infection, anaphylaxis, irritant exposure)
