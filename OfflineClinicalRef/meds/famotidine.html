Famotidine

Stocked on board

  • Famotidine 20mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Dyspepsia / heartburn / acid indigestion (intermittent)
    • 20 mg PO once daily as needed OR 20 mg PO before a known trigger meal (per product directions/guidance)
  • GERD symptoms (short-term)
    • 20 mg PO twice daily (e.g., morning and bedtime) for a short course; reassess
  • Suspected gastritis/ulcer symptoms (selected cases; provider-directed)
    • 20 mg PO twice daily short-term; reassess and consider escalation if red flags or persistent symptoms

Indications

  • Relief/prevention of heartburn, acid indigestion, sour/upset stomach due to excess acid
  • Symptomatic GERD (short-term management)
  • Selected acid-related conditions where H2-blocker is appropriate (provider-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to famotidine or other H2-receptor antagonists
  • Renal impairment: dose reduction may be required (risk of CNS effects in significant impairment)
  • Red flags requiring reassessment/escalation: GI bleeding, persistent vomiting, unexplained weight loss, progressive dysphagia, severe/persistent pain
  • Consider drug interactions/absorption issues for drugs needing acidic gastric pH (rare onboard relevance; use judgment)

Adverse effects

  • Headache, dizziness
  • Constipation or diarrhea
  • Rare: confusion (higher risk in older adults or renal impairment)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; may take with or without food
  • For nocturnal symptoms, bedtime dosing may help
  • If using antacids as well, separate if needed for symptom control (local practice)

  • Symptom response within 24â€“48 hours
  • Ongoing need (avoid indefinite use without reassessment)
  • Watch for CNS effects in older adults or those with renal impairment
  • Screen for red flags and escalate evaluation when present

At-sea considerations

  • Limited diagnostics: treat symptomatically but maintain a low threshold to reassess for GI bleed or cardiac mimics of epigastric pain
  • Document trigger patterns (diet, NSAID use, alcohol, stress) and consider risk reduction onboard
  • If frequent NSAID use (e.g., ibuprofen/naproxen), reassess GI risk and consider gastroprotection strategy per local guidance
