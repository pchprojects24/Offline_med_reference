Dimenhydrinate

Stocked on board

  • Dimenhydrinate 50mg Injection 5mL
  • Dimenhydrinate 50mg Tablets (Prepack 25-30)
  • Dimenhydrinate Caplets

Adult dosing

  • Motion sickness (prevention/treatment)
    • 50–100 mg PO every 4 hours as needed (max 400 mg in 24 hours)
    • For prevention: take at least 30 minutes before travel (ideally 1–2 hours before)
  • Nausea/vomiting (moderate–severe; when oral not tolerated or faster onset needed)
    • 25–50 mg IV/IM every 4–6 hours as needed
    • If older/frail: consider 12.5 mg IV/IM every 4–6 hours as needed (delirium risk)
  • Opioid-associated nausea (protocol-dependent)
    • Same as nausea/vomiting dosing above; reassess need as opioid dosing changes

Indications

  • Prevention and treatment of nausea/vomiting/dizziness due to motion sickness
  • Symptomatic relief of moderate–severe nausea and vomiting
  • Antiemetic option for opioid-associated nausea (context/protocol dependent)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to dimenhydrinate/diphenhydramine (and related antihistamines)
  • Sedation / impaired performance risk (esp. watchkeepers, machinery, ladders, weapons handling)
  • Anticholinergic cautions: glaucoma/increased ocular pressure, urinary retention/BPH, severe constipation/ileus risk
  • Caution in asthma/COPD (drying/thickened secretions), cardiovascular disease, and older adults (delirium/falls)
  • Avoid combining with other sedatives/CNS depressants when possible (alcohol, benzodiazepines, opioids)

Adverse effects

  • Drowsiness, dizziness, impaired coordination
  • Dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention, constipation
  • Confusion/delirium (higher risk in older/frail; dose related)

Preparation & administration

  • PO: use lowest effective dose; counsel re: sedation
  • IV/IM: administer per local protocol; monitor for sedation/hypotension
  • Reassess after dose; avoid stacking with other sedating agents unless necessary

Monitoring

  • Mental status/sedation level; fall risk
  • BP/HR if giving parenteral doses
  • Symptom response (nausea score, ability to hydrate/retain PO)

At-sea considerations

  • Favor non-sedating strategies when feasible (hydration, small sips, environmental control); use lowest effective dose
  • Document dose/time clearly; avoid double-dosing when multiple providers involved
  • For ongoing vomiting: consider dehydration risk and need for escalation/IV fluids per context
