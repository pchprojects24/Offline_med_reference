Cardiac / Vascular

Aspirin

Stocked on board

  • Aspirin 325mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Suspected acute coronary syndrome / suspected MI (if no allergy/intolerance and no active major bleeding)

  • Chew and swallow 160–325 mg as early as possible (use what you have available; you stock 325 mg tablets)

Pain/fever (less commonly needed if acetaminophen/NSAIDs preferred)

  • 325–650 mg PO every 4–6 hours as needed (max daily dose depends on indication and patient risk; use local guidance)

Indications

  • Suspected acute myocardial infarction / unstable angina (antiplatelet effect)
  • Secondary prevention in known vascular disease (provider-directed)
  • Analgesic/antipyretic/anti-inflammatory uses (less common onboard vs other agents)

Contraindications / cautions

  • ASA hypersensitivity, ASA-exacerbated respiratory disease
  • Active significant bleeding or high bleeding risk; known bleeding disorder
  • Severe uncontrolled peptic ulcer disease / recent GI bleed (risk–benefit)
  • Caution with concurrent anticoagulants/antiplatelets and heavy alcohol use

Preparation & administration

  • For suspected MI: chew (do not just swallow whole) for faster effect
  • Avoid "double-dosing" if patient already took ASA—confirm history if possible

Monitoring

  • Watch for bleeding (GI symptoms, bruising) and allergic reaction/bronchospasm in sensitive patients

Adverse effects

  • Dyspepsia, nausea
  • GI bleeding/ulceration (dose/risk dependent)
  • Bronchospasm/hypersensitivity reactions (susceptible patients)

At-sea considerations

  • In chest pain pathways: prioritize early ASA when indicated; document dose/time clearly
  • If med history is unclear, use quick screening: allergy/intolerance + active bleeding history
  • Plan for limited diagnostics: treat high-risk presentations conservatively and escalate/evac when indicated
