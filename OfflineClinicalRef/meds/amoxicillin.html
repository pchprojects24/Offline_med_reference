Amoxicillin

Stocked on board

  • Amoxicillin 500mg Capsules

Adult dosing

  • Acute otitis media (adult)
    • 500 mg PO every 8 hours
  • Acute bacterial sinusitis (adult; when antibiotics indicated)
    • 500 mg PO every 8 hours OR 875 mg PO every 12 hours (if available locally)
    • If using 500 mg capsules onboard: 500 mg PO every 8 hours
  • Streptococcal pharyngitis (GAS) (adult; if treated)
    • 500 mg PO every 12 hours for 10 days
  • Community-acquired pneumonia (mild outpatient; when amoxicillin appropriate)
    • 1 g PO every 8 hours (i.e., 2 x 500 mg capsules every 8 hours) for ~5–7 days (clinical response dependent)
  • Endocarditis prophylaxis (only if specifically indicated by guideline/medical direction)
    • Dosing varies by guideline; follow local protocol/medical direction

Indications

  • Susceptible bacterial infections (respiratory, ENT, skin/soft tissue, selected GU) where amoxicillin is appropriate
  • Streptococcal pharyngitis (to prevent complications when treated)
  • Selected CAP cases (when pathogen coverage is appropriate)

Contraindications / cautions

  • History of immediate hypersensitivity to penicillins (and caution with cephalosporin cross-reactivity depending on reaction)
  • Suspected/confirmed infectious mononucleosis (rash risk with aminopenicillins)
  • Dose adjustment required in significant renal impairment
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea risk: reassess if significant diarrhea develops during/after therapy
  • Interactions/bleeding risk: INR may increase with warfarin (monitor if applicable)

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea), rash
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (urticaria → anaphylaxis)
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea (important but uncommon)

Preparation & administration

  • Give with water; take at evenly spaced intervals
  • If GI upset occurs, taking with food may improve tolerance (absorption considerations vary by product; prioritize adherence/tolerance onboard)
  • Counsel: finish the prescribed course unless told otherwise; do not share leftover antibiotics

Monitoring

  • Clinical response within 48–72 hours (symptoms, fever, function)
  • Allergy/rash; stop and seek help if signs of anaphylaxis
  • Diarrhea severity (consider C. difficile if severe/persistent)
  • If prolonged course or high-risk patient: consider renal function monitoring when feasible

At-sea considerations

  • Confirm indication and avoid antibiotics for likely viral/self-limited illness when possible (stewardship)
  • If diagnostics are limited, reassess early non-response and consider alternate diagnosis or need for escalation/evac
  • Document start date, dose, planned duration, and allergy status clearly
