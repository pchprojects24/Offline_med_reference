ACLS Algorithm

Post–Cardiac Arrest Care (ROSC)

Entry criteria

  • Return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC) after cardiac arrest
  • Palpable pulse with organized rhythm and signs of perfusion

Immediate actions (first 2–5 minutes)

  • Confirm ROSC:
    • Check pulse, blood pressure, mental status, ETCO₂ rise (if available)
  • Airway and breathing:
    • Maintain airway; provide oxygen as needed
    • Avoid both hypoxemia and unnecessary hyperoxemia (titrate oxygen once reliable SpO₂ available)
    • Ensure adequate ventilation; avoid hyperventilation
    • If advanced airway present: confirm placement with waveform capnography
  • Circulation:
    • Establish/confirm IV/IO access
    • Treat hypotension promptly (fluids as appropriate; start vasopressor infusion per local protocol if needed)
  • Monitoring:
    • Continuous ECG, pulse oximetry, capnography if available
    • Temperature, glucose, and frequent vitals

Core priorities (stabilize first, then diagnose)

1) Optimize oxygenation and ventilation

  • Target physiologic ventilation; avoid excessive ventilation rates/volumes
  • If available, use ABG/VBG and ETCO₂ trends to guide ventilation

2) Optimize hemodynamics

  • Aim for adequate perfusion pressure; treat hypotension early
  • Consider causes: ongoing shock, bleeding, cardiogenic causes, sepsis, etc.
  • Use fluids and vasopressors/inotropes per local protocol and clinical picture

3) 12-lead ECG and treat time-critical causes

  • Obtain a 12-lead ECG as soon as feasible
  • If STEMI/ischemia suspected: activate local pathway/consultation and plan definitive care
  • Consider pulmonary embolism, tamponade, tension pneumothorax, toxins, and other reversible causes based on context

4) Targeted temperature management / fever prevention

  • Prevent and treat fever after ROSC
  • Consider targeted temperature strategies per local protocol and patient eligibility (VERIFY exact targets/timing per system)

5) Neurologic and sedation considerations

  • Assess mental status; protect airway if not protecting
  • Provide analgesia/sedation as clinically required (especially if ventilated)
  • Avoid premature prognostication early after ROSC

6) Glucose and metabolic issues

  • Check glucose and correct significant hypo-/hyperglycemia per local protocol
  • Correct major electrolyte derangements (e.g., K+, Mg2+) and acid-base issues as indicated

7) Ongoing reassessment

  • Reassess ABCs frequently
  • Monitor for recurrent arrest or unstable rhythms
  • Treat dysrhythmias per ACLS pathways (bradycardia/tachycardia)

Exit paths

  • Stable ROSC with identified disposition → continue post-arrest bundle, arrange transfer/definitive care as appropriate
  • Recurrent arrest → return immediately to appropriate arrest algorithm (shockable or non-shockable)
  • Persistent instability or uncertain cause → escalate early (consult/MEDEVAC triggers per local context)
