ACLS Special Situation

Suspected Stroke (acute)

Entry criteria (treat as stroke until proven otherwise)

  • Sudden onset of new focal neurologic deficit, e.g.:
    • Face droop, arm/leg weakness, numbness
    • Speech/language change (aphasia, dysarthria)
    • Vision loss/diplopia
    • Ataxia, severe imbalance
    • Neglect, new confusion (especially with focal signs)
  • Consider posterior circulation stroke with: vertigo + gait ataxia, dysphagia, diplopia, unilateral weakness, or "worst imbalance" out of proportion

Immediate priorities (first moments)

  • Call for help; activate "stroke pathway" / consult early
  • ABCs:
    • Airway protection if reduced LOC, vomiting, or aspiration risk
    • Oxygen only as needed (treat hypoxemia)
  • Check glucose immediately (treat hypoglycemia as a mimic)
  • Establish IV access; draw bloods per local protocol (do not delay transfer/consult for labs)
  • Continuous monitoring: ECG, SpO₂, BP
  • Keep NPO until swallow screen completed (or speech/SLP assessment when available)

Time is critical — document "LAST KNOWN WELL" (LKW)

  • Determine exact time patient was last known at baseline/normal
  • If wake-up stroke: LKW is when last seen normal before sleep (unless advanced imaging guidance available)
  • Document:
    • Symptom onset / LKW time
    • Witnessed vs unwitnessed
    • Progression/steps vs sudden onset

Quick focused assessment (minimum dataset)

  • FAST-type screen:
    • Face, Arm, Speech, Time (and note LKW)
  • Focused neuro:
    • Side and severity of weakness, speech/language, vision, neglect, gait
  • Screen for large vessel occlusion features (practical red flags):
    • Dense hemiplegia, gaze deviation, severe aphasia/neglect, profound visual field loss
    • If suspected: treat as time-critical for EVT-capable centre (VERIFY local triage criteria)

History "must-have" questions (for thrombolysis/EVT eligibility)

  • Anticoagulants / antiplatelets:
    • Warfarin, DOACs, heparin; last dose time if known
  • Bleeding risk / recent events (VERIFY local exclusion list):
    • Recent major surgery/bleed, intracranial pathology, recent head trauma, pregnancy/postpartum considerations
  • Baseline function and pre-morbid status
  • Seizure at onset? (stroke mimic vs stroke-with-seizure)
  • Headache + neck pain + trauma? (consider dissection/SAH)
  • Fever/meningism? (consider infection)
  • Migrainous aura history? (consider migraine mimic)

Blood pressure approach (do not create harm)

  • Avoid aggressive BP lowering unless clearly indicated
  • If candidate for thrombolysis: BP may need to meet protocol thresholds (VERIFY exact targets/agents)
  • If not a thrombolysis candidate: permissive hypertension is often acceptable (VERIFY local guidance)
  • Treat hypotension promptly (hypotension worsens cerebral perfusion)

Imaging and definitive therapies (hospital pathway reminders)

  • Brain imaging is required to distinguish ischemic vs hemorrhagic stroke
  • IV thrombolysis:
    • Generally within 4.5 hours of LKW for eligible patients; extended windows may exist with advanced imaging and stroke expertise (VERIFY local pathway)
  • Endovascular thrombectomy (EVT):
    • For selected patients with large vessel occlusion, may be considered up to 24 hours from LKW using advanced imaging selection (VERIFY local pathway)

Shipboard / austere considerations (operational)

  • If no immediate access to CT/CTA:
    • Prioritize rapid consult and transport/evac planning early
    • Your job is to stabilize ABCs, document LKW, screen for mimics, and package information for receiving team
  • Consider evacuation triggers (context-dependent; VERIFY with chain/protocol):
    • Disabling deficit, suspected LVO, rapidly worsening symptoms, decreased LOC, airway risk

What NOT to do (common pitfalls)

  • Do not delay escalation/transport while "watching for improvement"
  • Do not give antiplatelet therapy before hemorrhage is excluded by imaging (unless local protocol explicitly directs otherwise)
  • Do not feed the patient until swallow screen completed
  • Do not normalize BP aggressively without a clear indication

Exit paths

  • Confirmed/suspected stroke with time-critical features → activate stroke centre pathway / arrange transfer or MEDEVAC as appropriate
  • Mimic identified and treated (e.g., hypoglycemia) → reassess neurologic deficits; still escalate if deficits persist
  • Deterioration (airway compromise, declining LOC) → stabilize and escalate urgently
