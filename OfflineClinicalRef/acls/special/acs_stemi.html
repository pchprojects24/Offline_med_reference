ACLS Special Situation

ACS / STEMI pathway

Entry criteria (suspect ACS)

  • Chest discomfort/pressure (may radiate to arm, neck, jaw, back) OR anginal equivalent:
    • Shortness of breath, diaphoresis, nausea/vomiting, unexplained fatigue, syncope/near-syncope
  • Maintain suspicion even if pain is atypical or reproducible on palpation (does not fully exclude ischemia)

Immediate actions (first moments)

  • Call for help; activate local "chest pain / STEMI" pathway early
  • ABCs:
    • Airway and breathing support as needed
    • Oxygen only if hypoxemic (target adequate SpO₂; avoid routine oxygen if normal)
  • Place on monitor/defib; obtain vitals; establish IV access
  • 12-lead ECG as early as feasible; repeat if initial ECG is nondiagnostic but symptoms persist

Critical history (minimum dataset)

  • Symptom onset time and progression; current pain score
  • Cardiac history (prior MI/PCI/CABG), risk factors
  • Current meds (especially anticoagulants)
  • Allergy/intolerance to aspirin
  • Nitroglycerin use: prescribed? effective?
  • Contraindication screen for nitrates:
    • Hypotension (low SBP), marked bradycardia/tachycardia
    • Recent PDE-5 inhibitor use (e.g., sildenafil/tadalafil) (VERIFY time windows per protocol)
    • Suspected right-ventricular infarct / inferior STEMI with preload dependence (VERIFY local criteria)

Immediate medications (early, if no contraindications)

  • Aspirin (ASA)
    • Chew and swallow as early as possible if no allergy/intolerance
    • Dose: use your local protocol / available product (VERIFY; common practice is 160–325 mg)
  • Nitroglycerin (for ongoing ischemic discomfort, if hemodynamically stable)
    • Give only if no nitrate contraindications and BP/HR acceptable per protocol (VERIFY)
    • Reassess BP and symptoms after each dose; stop if hypotension, worsening symptoms, or concern for RV infarct
  • Analgesia/antiemetic as needed per local protocol (avoid causing hypotension)

Interpret ECG and route care

  • STEMI suspected (ST elevation or equivalent pattern):
    • Treat as time-critical: notify receiving facility early; expedite reperfusion pathway selection
    • Minimize delays; plan direct-to-PCI strategy when applicable
  • Non-ST elevation ACS suspected (NSTEMI/unstable angina):
    • Continue monitoring, repeat ECGs, manage symptoms, and follow local chest pain pathway for destination and therapies

Complication watch-outs (treat immediately)

  • Cardiogenic shock / pulmonary edema:
    • Support oxygenation/ventilation; treat hypotension; consider NIPPV if available and appropriate
  • Dangerous dysrhythmias:
    • Treat per ACLS pathways (bradycardia/tachycardia)
  • Consider alternate life threats if presentation suggests:
    • Aortic dissection, pulmonary embolism, tension pneumothorax (VERIFY local red flags)

Shipboard / austere considerations

  • Your job is to: stabilize ABCs, obtain/interpret 12-lead if capable, give early ASA if appropriate, avoid harmful nitrates, and trigger early consult/MEDEVAC when indicated
  • Early escalation triggers (context-dependent; VERIFY):
    • STEMI pattern, refractory pain, hemodynamic instability, significant arrhythmia, heart failure/shock

Exit paths

  • STEMI pathway activated → rapid transport/transfer plan + ongoing monitoring/support
  • ACS suspected without STEMI → follow local chest pain pathway; repeat ECGs; monitor for deterioration
  • Deterioration to cardiac arrest → transition to ACLS arrest algorithms
