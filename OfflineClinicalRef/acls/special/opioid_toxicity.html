ACLS Special Situation

Opioid toxicity / Respiratory arrest

Entry criteria (suspect opioid tox)

  • Depressed level of consciousness with respiratory depression or apnea
  • May have: miosis, bradypnea, hypoxemia, cyanosis
  • Consider mixed/unknown ingestion if atypical features (agitation, seizures, hyperthermia) or poor response to naloxone

Immediate actions (first moments)

  • Call for help; assign roles
  • Airway/Breathing:
    • Open airway; suction as needed
    • Provide high-flow oxygen as needed
    • Ventilate with BVM immediately if bradypneic or apneic
    • Apply waveform capnography if available (especially once ventilating)
  • Circulation:
    • Check pulse, BP; attach monitor/defib
    • Establish IV access (IO if needed)

Core approach (treat the breathing first)

1) Ventilate/oxygenate

  • If apnea or inadequate respirations: BVM ventilation is priority
  • Avoid hyperventilation; aim for visible chest rise and improving oxygenation/ETCO₂

2) Naloxone (to restore adequate breathing)

  • Goal: adequate spontaneous ventilation (not full arousal)
  • Preferred route depends on what you can do quickly (IV/IO vs IM vs IN)
  • Adult dosing (VERIFY your local protocol and product concentration):
    • If IV/IO available:
      • Titrate in small increments to effect (e.g., 0.04 mg → 0.1 mg → 0.2 mg → 0.4 mg, repeating as needed)
    • If no IV/IO:
      • IM or IN naloxone per product/device instructions (common IN kits are 4 mg per spray; IM auto-injector dosing varies)
  • If no response after appropriate doses:
    • Continue ventilation and broaden differential (mixed tox, CNS event, metabolic cause)

3) Reassess continuously

  • Respiratory rate, SpO₂, ETCO₂, mental status, airway protection
  • Re-dose naloxone if respiratory depression recurs (naloxone may wear off before the opioid)

Airway/ventilation checkpoints

  • If patient cannot maintain airway, has recurrent apnea, or needs repeated naloxone:
    • Prepare for advanced airway based on your capability and context
  • If advanced airway placed:
    • Confirm with waveform capnography
    • Maintain appropriate ventilation; monitor ETCO₂

Complications / cautions

  • Acute withdrawal can occur (agitation, vomiting, hypertension, tachycardia)
    • Titrate naloxone to breathing, not wakefulness
    • Be ready for aspiration risk; position and suction
  • Consider co-ingestants (benzodiazepines, alcohol, stimulants) if incomplete response

Exit paths

  • Improved and stable with adequate ventilation → observe for recurrence; plan disposition and follow-up
  • Recurrent respiratory depression → repeat naloxone and consider infusion per local protocol (VERIFY)
  • Cardiac arrest → begin ACLS arrest algorithm (treat as arrest; naloxone should not delay CPR/defib)
