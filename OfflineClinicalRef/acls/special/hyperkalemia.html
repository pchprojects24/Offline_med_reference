ACLS Special Situation

Hyperkalemia

Entry criteria (suspect hyperK+)

  • Known/suspected elevated potassium OR clinical scenario with risk:
    • Renal failure, missed dialysis, crush/rhabdo, severe acidosis, certain meds
  • Concerning ECG features may include (examples):
    • Peaked T waves, PR prolongation, QRS widening
    • Loss of P waves, sine-wave pattern
    • Bradycardia, ventricular dysrhythmias

Immediate actions (first moments)

  • Place on monitor/defib; obtain vitals
  • Establish IV access (consider two lines if feasible)
  • Obtain 12-lead ECG as soon as possible (do not delay treatment if unstable)
  • If unstable rhythm, severe bradycardia, or wide-complex changes: treat immediately

Core treatment goals (in order)

  1. Stabilize the myocardium
  2. Shift potassium into cells
  3. Remove potassium from the body
  4. Treat the underlying cause

1) Myocardial stabilization (if ECG changes or instability)

  • Calcium to stabilize cardiac membrane (VERIFY local choice/dose/concentration):
    • Calcium gluconate IV (common) OR calcium chloride IV (more elemental calcium; preferred via central line if used)
  • Reassess ECG and clinical status after calcium
  • Repeat calcium if ECG abnormalities persist or recur (VERIFY local repeat timing)

2) Shift potassium into cells

  • Insulin + glucose (VERIFY dosing and glucose thresholds per local protocol)
    • Give dextrose if needed to prevent hypoglycemia
    • Monitor glucose closely (hypoglycemia risk)
  • Beta-agonist (adjunct)
    • Nebulized salbutamol/albuterol can help shift K+ intracellularly (VERIFY dosing)
  • Sodium bicarbonate
    • Consider if significant metabolic acidosis is suspected/confirmed (VERIFY indications/dose)

3) Remove potassium from the body

  • Loop diuretic + fluids if appropriate and patient can make urine (VERIFY)
  • Potassium binders (slower onset; role depends on local availability/protocol) (VERIFY)
  • Definitive removal:
    • Dialysis if available/indicated (not shipboard, but triggers early escalation/transfer planning)

Rhythm-specific notes (practical)

  • Hyperkalemia can cause bradycardia and "pseudo-infarct" ECG patterns
  • If bradycardic and unstable with suspected hyperkalemia:
    • Prioritize calcium + shifting therapies; standard bradycardia meds may be less effective until K+ addressed
  • If ventricular dysrhythmia/arrest occurs:
    • Follow ACLS pathway PLUS treat hyperkalemia concurrently (calcium + shifting therapy)

Reassessment / monitoring

  • Repeat ECG after interventions
  • Monitor BP, rhythm, and glucose (especially after insulin)
  • Watch for recurrence if underlying cause not corrected

Exit paths

  • ECG and hemodynamics improved → continue monitoring, address cause, plan definitive care/transfer if needed
  • Persistent ECG instability → repeat stabilization/shifting steps per protocol and escalate early
  • Deterioration to cardiac arrest → transition to arrest algorithms while treating hyperkalemia concurrently
