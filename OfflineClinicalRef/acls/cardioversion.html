ACLS Algorithm

Electrical Cardioversion (synchronized)

Purpose

  • Restore organized rhythm in tachyarrhythmias causing instability, or when appropriate in stable patients per clinical context
  • Use synchronized cardioversion for organized tachycardias WITH a pulse (when indicated)

Immediate safety checks

  • Confirm patient has a pulse (if pulseless → use defibrillation + CPR per arrest algorithm)
  • Attach monitor/defib; place pads (anterolateral or anteroposterior)
  • Ensure oxygenation/ventilation and IV access
  • If time permits: analgesia/sedation and airway readiness (VERIFY local meds/doses)
  • Confirm "SYNC" mode is ON and markers align with R waves before delivering shock
  • Clear patient and staff; ensure no one is touching bed/rails/patient

When to cardiovert (common ACLS triggers)

  • Unstable tachycardia with pulse:
    • Hypotension
    • Acutely altered mental status
    • Signs of shock
    • Ischemic chest discomfort
    • Acute heart failure

Rhythm-directed approach (practical)

  • Narrow, regular tachycardia (SVT) → synchronized cardioversion if unstable or refractory
  • Narrow, irregular tachycardia (AF/flutter with variable block) → synchronized cardioversion if unstable
  • Wide, regular tachycardia (monomorphic VT with pulse) → synchronized cardioversion if unstable
  • Wide, irregular or polymorphic tachycardia:
    • If polymorphic VT suspected or synchronization not reliable → do NOT cardiovert in SYNC mode; treat as defibrillation per local protocol (VERIFY)

Energy selection (biphasic)

  • Use manufacturer-recommended energy settings
  • If unsure, start lower and escalate; escalate energy if unsuccessful
  • If rhythm is AF and initial attempt fails, escalate appropriately (VERIFY local energy ranges/protocol)

Procedure steps

  1. Identify rhythm and confirm indication (unstable vs stable-refractory)
  2. Prepare:
    • Pads on, IV access, oxygen, suction available
    • Consider sedation/analgesia if time allows
  3. Set to SYNC and confirm sync markers
  4. Charge to selected energy
  5. Clear and deliver synchronized shock
  6. Immediately reassess:
    • Rhythm, pulse, BP, symptoms
    • Repeat synchronized shock with higher energy if needed
  7. After conversion or if persistent tachycardia:
    • Obtain 12-lead ECG when feasible
    • Treat underlying cause and consider antiarrhythmic strategy per rhythm and local protocol

Post-cardioversion priorities

  • Monitor for recurrence
  • Evaluate for ischemia, electrolyte issues, toxicologic causes, or structural heart disease
  • If AF/flutter: anticoagulation considerations are outside the immediate ACLS algorithm; follow local pathway (VERIFY)

Exit paths

  • Rhythm converted and patient stable → monitor and manage underlying cause/disposition
  • Cardioversion unsuccessful → escalate energy, consider alternate rhythm diagnosis, consult/transfer as appropriate
  • Deterioration to cardiac arrest → transition to shockable/non-shockable arrest algorithm
