ACLS Algorithm

Bradycardia (with pulse)

Entry criteria

  • Bradycardia with a palpable pulse (typically HR < 50/min) AND concern for clinical instability
  • Treat the patient, not the number

Immediate assessment

  • ABCs: airway, breathing, oxygenation, circulation
  • Attach monitor/defib; obtain BP and SpO₂
  • Establish IV access
  • 12-lead ECG as soon as feasible (do not delay treatment if unstable)

Decide: symptomatic (unstable) vs not

  • If NO signs of poor perfusion → monitor, evaluate cause, treat underlying issue
  • If symptomatic/unstable (treat now), examples:
    • Hypotension
    • Altered mental status
    • Signs of shock
    • Ischemic chest discomfort
    • Acute heart failure

Core actions for symptomatic/unstable bradycardia

1) Supportive care

  • Maintain airway; give oxygen as needed
  • Consider fluid bolus if appropriate (e.g., relative hypovolemia)
  • Prepare for pacing early if high-grade block suspected or rapid deterioration

2) First-line medication

  • Atropine IV
    • 1 mg IV bolus
    • Repeat every 3–5 minutes as needed
    • Max total 3 mg
  • If atropine is unlikely to work (e.g., high-grade AV block) → move early to pacing and/or infusion support

3) If atropine ineffective OR patient is crashing

  • Transcutaneous pacing (TCP)
    • Apply pads, start pacing per device workflow
    • Provide analgesia/sedation as clinically appropriate if time permits
  • Vasopressor/inotrope infusion support (choose ONE per local protocol):
    • Epinephrine infusion: 2–10 mcg/min (titrate to response) [VERIFY local range/prep]
    • Dopamine infusion: 5–20 mcg/kg/min (titrate to response) [VERIFY local range/prep]

Concurrent priorities (while treating)

  • Identify and correct reversible causes:
    • Hypoxia
    • Hypothermia
    • Drug/toxin effect (beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, digoxin, opioids/sedatives)
    • Electrolytes (e.g., hyperkalemia)
    • Myocardial ischemia/infarction
    • Increased vagal tone
    • Conduction disease / high-grade AV block
  • Review ECG for:
    • Sinus bradycardia vs junctional rhythm
    • AV blocks (especially 2nd-degree type II or 3rd-degree)
    • Wide QRS brady rhythms suggesting infranodal disease

Exit paths

  • Stabilized and symptoms resolve → monitor, treat underlying cause, disposition/consult as appropriate
  • Persistent instability despite atropine → pacing and/or infusion; early escalation/consult
  • Deteriorates to cardiac arrest → transition to arrest algorithm
