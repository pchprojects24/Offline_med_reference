ACLS Algorithm

Tachycardia (with pulse)

Entry criteria

  • Tachycardia with a palpable pulse (often HR ≥ 150/min), requiring evaluation and management
  • Treat the patient and stability first; heart rate alone is not the only trigger

Immediate assessment (first moments)

  • ABCs: airway, breathing, oxygenation, circulation
  • Attach monitor/defib; obtain BP and SpO₂; establish IV access
  • 12-lead ECG as soon as feasible (do not delay treatment if unstable)
  • Identify:
    • Is the patient unstable?
    • QRS width: narrow (< 0.12 s) vs wide (≥ 0.12 s)
    • Rhythm regular vs irregular

Decide: unstable vs stable

  • Unstable (treat now) if tachyarrhythmia is causing:
    • Hypotension
    • Acutely altered mental status
    • Signs of shock
    • Ischemic chest discomfort
    • Acute heart failure

If UNSTABLE

  • Immediate synchronized cardioversion
    • Provide analgesia/sedation if time permits (VERIFY local meds/doses)
    • If rhythm is irregular wide-complex and polymorphic, do NOT synchronize; treat as defibrillation per arrest/unstable polymorphic VT pathway (VERIFY local protocol)
  • After cardioversion:
    • Reassess ABCs, rhythm, and underlying cause
    • Obtain/confirm 12-lead ECG when feasible and treat precipitant

If STABLE

  • Determine QRS width and regularity, then treat accordingly

A) Narrow QRS (< 0.12 s)

1) Regular narrow-complex tachycardia (likely SVT)

  • Vagal maneuvers (if appropriate)
  • Adenosine:
    • 6 mg rapid IV push + flush
    • If needed: 12 mg rapid IV push + flush
  • If persists or diagnosis unclear: consider rate-control/antiarrhythmic strategy per rhythm and seek expert help as needed

2) Irregular narrow-complex tachycardia (likely AF/flutter with variable block)

  • Focus on rate control and underlying cause
  • Avoid adenosine as a "treatment" for irregular rhythms (it may help diagnosis briefly but does not treat AF)
  • If concern for pre-excitation (very rapid, irregular, changing QRS morphology) → avoid AV nodal blockers and seek urgent expert guidance (VERIFY local protocol)

B) Wide QRS (≥ 0.12 s)

1) Regular wide-complex tachycardia (likely monomorphic VT)

  • Consider adenosine ONLY if rhythm is regular and monomorphic and SVT with aberrancy is suspected
  • Antiarrhythmic infusion options for stable wide-complex tachycardia (choose ONE per local protocol):
    • Procainamide infusion (stop if hypotension, QRS widens significantly, arrhythmia suppressed, or max dose reached) (VERIFY local prep)
    • Amiodarone infusion (VERIFY local prep)
    • Sotalol infusion (avoid in prolonged QT) (VERIFY local prep)
  • Seek expert consultation if any uncertainty

2) Irregular wide-complex tachycardia

  • Differential includes AF with aberrancy, pre-excited AF, or polymorphic VT
  • If polymorphic VT suspected or patient deteriorating → treat urgently (may require defibrillation) (VERIFY local protocol)
  • Avoid AV nodal blockers if pre-excited AF is suspected; seek expert consultation urgently

Concurrent priorities (all stable patients)

  • Treat precipitating causes (e.g., hypoxia, fever/sepsis, pain, dehydration, stimulant/drug effect, electrolyte abnormalities, ischemia)
  • Correct major K+/Mg2+ derangements if present (VERIFY local targets)
  • If any clinical deterioration → treat as UNSTABLE immediately

Exit paths

  • Rhythm controlled and patient stable → observe, investigate cause, disposition/consultation as appropriate
  • Unstable at any point → synchronized cardioversion (or defibrillation for polymorphic/irregular wide-complex as indicated) and escalate
  • Deteriorates to cardiac arrest → transition to arrest algorithms
